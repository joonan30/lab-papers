# Whole-genome sequencing analyses suggest novel genetic factors associated with Alzheimer's disease and a cumulative effects model for risk liability

## Bibliographic Information
- **Authors**: Jun Pyo Kim, Minyoung Cho, Chanhee Kim (co-first authors) and others. Corresponding authors: Sang Won Seo (sangwonseo@empas.com), Joon-Yong An (joonan30@korea.ac.kr), Hong-Hee Won (wonhh@skku.edu). (Affiliations: Samsung Medical Center / Sungkyunkwan University, Korea University, Sungkyunkwan University)
- **Journal / Year**: Nature Communications, 2025; 16:4870 (Received 2024-05-26, Accepted 2025-05-08, Published 2025-05-26)
- **PMID / DOI**: PMID 40419521 / https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-59949-y
- **Article Type**: Original Article — large cohort-based whole-genome sequencing (WGS) genetic association study

## One-Line Summary
An integrated analysis of high-depth WGS from a Korean Alzheimer's disease (AD) cohort of 1,559 individuals — jointly examining common variants (including novel loci such as APCDD1), rare coding/non-coding variants, and structural variants (CNV, STR) — showing that diverse genetic factors act **cumulatively** on cognitive impairment and amyloid-beta (Aβ) accumulation.

## Research Summary
Alzheimer's disease (AD) is the leading cause of dementia, affecting more than 57 million people worldwide, and is a complex neurodegenerative disorder with heritability of roughly 60–80%. Prior large-scale GWAS have identified 75 AD-associated loci but explain only about 15% of phenotypic variance, and most studies have been concentrated in European populations, creating a need for research in diverse ancestral groups and for exploration of rare and non-coding variants. To fill this gap, the authors performed high-depth WGS in a Korean AD cohort and broadly surveyed not only common variants but also poorly characterized rare non-coding variants and structural variants (CNV, STR).

The study analyzed 1,559 individuals who passed QC (mean 30× depth WGS) out of 1,824 recruited from the K-ROAD (Korea Registries to Overcome and Accelerate Dementia, 2017–2023) project. Clinical diagnoses comprised 655 cognitively unimpaired (CU), 590 mild cognitive impairment (MCI), and 314 dementia of the Alzheimer's type (DAT). Using Aβ PET imaging (centiloid threshold of 40), 925 were classified as Aβ-positive and 634 as Aβ-negative. Taking clinical diagnosis and Aβ positivity as the core outcome variables, the authors integrated single-variant association analysis of common variants, gene-based analysis of rare coding variants, a category-wide association study (CWAS) of rare non-coding variants, and structural variant (CNV, STR) association analysis.

Among the key results, common-variant GWAS identified the previously unreported **APCDD1** locus (rs28372356) as significantly associated with clinical diagnosis (alongside APOE), and this signal was replicated in Korean and Japanese East Asian cohorts. The Aβ GWAS identified, in addition to APOE, two suggestive loci — **SAMD3** and **PTPRD** — and gene-based analysis showed suggestive gene burden of **DRC7** for Aβ positivity. Rare non-coding variant analysis (CWAS) revealed that a cluster of variants located in excitatory neuron–specific cis-regulatory elements (CREs) (Cluster 25, C25) was associated with cognitive impairment. Structural variant analysis showed that short tandem repeat (STR) expansions were associated with increased AD risk, and a CNV deletion at the **HPSE2** locus showed a borderline association with Aβ.

Finally, when diverse genetic factors (APOE ε4, polygenic risk score PRS, rare non-coding variants, structural variants) were integrated, the explained variance (incremental r²) for clinical diagnosis increased from 12.8% using European loci alone, to 14.7% with the addition of Korean loci, and to 16.6% with the addition of rare/structural variants; for Aβ it rose from 7.5% to 9.8% to 10.7%. In particular, **APOE ε4 carriers who simultaneously carried a high polygenic burden or structural variants showed more severe cognitive impairment and higher Aβ levels**, supporting a "cumulative effects model" in which genetic factors act additively on AD risk liability.

## Methods & Technologies
- **Sequencing**: gDNA extracted from blood using the QIAamp DNA Mini Kit, TruSeq DNA PCR-Free libraries, and mean 30× paired-end WGS on NovaSeq 6000. hg38 reference genome.
- **Cohort scale**: 1,824 recruited in K-ROAD → 1,559 after QC (CU 655 / MCI 590 / DAT 314; Aβ-positive 925 / negative 634). Participation from 25 university hospitals.
- **Alignment and variant calling**: Trim Galore (trimming) → BWA-MEM (alignment) → GATK MarkDuplicates, BaseRecalibrator, HaplotypeCaller, CombineGVCFs pipeline.
- **QC**: Variant and sample QC based on Hail (v0.2.68), PLINK v1.90 (PCA), KING 2.0 (kinship). VQSR, LCR filter, allele balance, GQ≥20, depth filters, HWE, etc.
- **Variant annotation**: VEP v108, LOFTEE (pLoF), dbNSFP (REVEL, MetaSVM, CADD, PolyPhen, SIFT), SpliceAI, AlphaMissense, PhyloP conservation scores.
- **Single-variant association analysis**: REGENIE (v2.2.4), LOOCV. Covariates: age, sex, sequencing batch, PC1–10. For clinical diagnosis, meta-analysis (METAL) was performed with an independent Korean chip-array cohort (n=863) and a Japanese cohort (n=938).
- **Gene-based analysis**: REGENIE, collapsing rare variants with MAF<1% using 7 masks (pLoF, missense combinations, splicing, CADD deleterious, etc.).
- **CWAS (rare non-coding variants)**: Using CWAS-Plus v1.2, 19,266,739 rare variants (MAF<0.1%) were categorized into 29,917 categories via 5 annotation types (variant type, gene set, conservation/constraint score, genomic region, functional annotation). Risk category clusters were identified using a burden test + permutation testing (1,000 permutations) + the DAWN hidden Markov random field model.
- **cis-eQTL / COLOC**: Cell-type-specific eQTL with tensorQTL, colocalization with COLOC (v3.2-1) (PP.H4>0.5). eQTL databases: GTEx, ROSMAP-MIT, MiGA, multi-ethnic, MetaBrain.
- **Gene prioritization**: 5 criteria — nearest gene, eQTL colocalization, publications (PubMed 'AD'/'brain'), scATAC-seq peak-to-gene linkage. Visualized with circlize.
- **Structural variants (CNV)**: Parliament2 (Manta, Delly, Lumpy, CNVnator in parallel) → SURVIVOR integration → SVTyper validation → AnnotSV annotation. Logistic regression, cytoband-based Bonferroni correction, samplot visual verification.
- **STR**: ExpansionHunter (v5.0.0), a panel of 312,302 polymorphic STRs (Guo et al.). Association testing under 3 models (length as a continuous variable / above-threshold / outlier (DBSCAN)). enrichGO pathway enrichment analysis.
- **PRS**: PRS-cs (v1.0.0), PLINK, with weights from European GWAS (clinical diagnosis n=487,511; Aβ n=11,816). PRS was also computed using the 3 lead SNPs from this study (rs28372356, rs6923619, rs78009495).
- **Single-cell validation**: Single-nucleus RNA-seq (Cell Ranger, SCANPY, Scrublet, Harmony) of Korean AD postmortem brain (DLPFC, 15 individuals), leveraging SEA-AD, Allen Brain Map, BTS (developmental trajectory), and the AD/Aging Brain Atlas.
- **Explained variance assessment**: Incremental r² computed with Nagelkerke's r².

## Key Genes / Proteins
- **APOE (ε4)**: The strongest AD risk gene. The strongest signal in both clinical diagnosis and Aβ GWAS. Modulates risk in combination with STR expansions.
- **APCDD1**: Novel locus discovered in this study (rs28372356, associated with clinical diagnosis). A Wnt signaling inhibitor; replicated across 3 East Asian cohorts.
- **SAMD3**: Suggestive locus for Aβ positivity (rs6923619). Expressed in astrocytes, with concordant eQTL and GWAS direction.
- **PTPRD**: Prioritized gene at the suggestive Aβ-positivity locus (rs78009495).
- **DRC7**: Suggestive gene-based burden for Aβ positivity (p=5.99×10⁻⁶). Dynein-related; increased expression in low-social-isolation excitatory neurons and diabetic astrocytes.
- **HPSE2**: A 309Kb CNV deletion showing borderline association with Aβ positivity (OR 1.60, p=0.0001).
- **VAPA**: Prioritized gene at rs28372356. Related to ER–plasma membrane junctions; expressed in astrocytes and increasing with AD progression.
- **TXNDC2**: Prioritized gene at rs28372356 (low expression across all brain cell types).
- **TMEM200A, EPB41L2, ARHGAP18**: Prioritized genes at rs6923619 (cell-type-specific differential expression in AD).

## Main Findings
- **APCDD1 (rs28372356, chr18:10326201, SNV)**: Associated with clinical diagnosis, OR 1.65 (95% CI 1.39–1.96), p=1.81×10⁻⁸. Consistent signal across 3 East Asian cohorts: Korean WGS (p=7.49×10⁻⁵), Korean chip (p=5.85×10⁻³), Japanese WGS (p=1.79×10⁻³). A novel locus not previously reported in European or East Asian studies.
- **HPSE2 (chr10:98736468–98737614, DEL)**: Associated with Aβ positivity, OR 1.60 (95% CI 1.25–1.99), p=0.0001. Borderline after cytoband Bonferroni correction (p=0.036).
- **Aβ GWAS suggestive loci**: SAMD3 (p=2.12×10⁻⁷), PTPRD (p=2.07×10⁻⁷) — not reported in European or East Asian studies.
- **Rare coding variants (DRC7)**: Suggestive gene burden for Aβ positivity (p=5.99×10⁻⁶). Among 25 top prioritized rare coding variants, 2 chr16 variants were predicted pathogenic by AlphaMissense.
- **Rare non-coding (CWAS)**: A cluster of excitatory neuron–specific CRE variants, Cluster 25 (FDR=8.7×10⁻⁵, RR=1.1). Aβ-positive individuals carrying C25 showed a significant reduction in K-MMSE score versus non-carriers (p=0.028). 39 C25 variants interacted with 109 genes via Hi-C, and 15 genes were differentially expressed in the Exc L2-3 CBLN2 LINC02306 subtype.
- **STR expansions**: Among 293,751 STRs, having ≥40 outliers was associated with Aβ positivity (OR 1.52, p=1.29×10⁻⁷, independent of APOE ε4). An STR near APOE (chr19:44921097–44921125-TTTA) was associated with Aβ positivity (p=7.48×10⁻³, concordant with prior European studies). Carrying ≥40 STR outliers was associated with significantly increased Aβ levels (overall p=0.0045, CU p=0.019). Genes harboring STR outliers were enriched for synaptic pathways such as synaptic membrane (GO:0097060).
- **Explained variance (incremental r²)**: Clinical diagnosis 12.8% (European) → 14.7% (+Korean) → 16.6% (+RV/SV). Aβ 7.5% → 9.8% → 10.7%.
- **Cumulative effects**: Compared with APOE ε4 alone, groups combining ε4+high PRS or ε4+structural variants (SV) showed more severe declines in Aβ levels and cognitive function. Phenotypic worsening accumulated when 2, 3, or 4 factors were carried simultaneously.

## Applications
- Discovery of East Asian (Korean)-specific and pan-ancestry AD risk loci and biomarkers → a foundation for improving multi-ethnic genetic risk prediction models.
- A multi-layered risk stratification framework integrating APOE ε4 + PRS + rare/structural variants, refining individual-level prediction of AD risk and Aβ accumulation.
- Provides a use case for applying WGS-based rare non-coding and structural variant (STR, CNV) analysis pipelines (e.g., CWAS-Plus) to neurodegenerative disease.
- Suggests mechanistic studies and potential therapeutic targets focused on excitatory neuron CREs and synaptic pathways (APCDD1/Wnt, DRC7/dynein, VAPA, etc.).

## Extensibility / Future Directions
- Because eQTL and single-cell transcriptomic data were mainly derived from European populations, construction of **large-scale gene expression datasets from diverse ancestral groups** is needed.
- Whether genetic variants identified in non-European populations actually perturb AD-related pathways requires **functional and molecular convergence validation**.
- CWAS single-category signals are underpowered given sample size and the complexity of non-coding variants → confirmation with **larger cohorts** and **CRISPR-based functional validation** is recommended.
- Findings such as CNV/HPSE deletions must be interpreted cautiously owing to limited statistical power and require validation in independent cohorts.
- Generalization of the cumulative effects model through expanded studies across diverse ancestral backgrounds.

## Disease Relevance
- Extends the genetic architecture of AD from a common-variant-centric view to a **cumulative risk model encompassing rare non-coding variants, structural variants, and polygenic burden**, more precisely explaining clinically heterogeneous AD (including atypical presentations) in combination with biomarkers such as Aβ.
- Suggests that excitatory neuron regulatory elements and synaptic pathways are involved in AD pathogenesis and in cognitive decline and reduced resilience.
- Demonstrates that risk is stratified among APOE ε4 carriers according to additional genetic burden (PRS, SV), providing a basis for personalized risk assessment.

## Novelty vs. Prior Work
- Whereas prior AD GWAS were centered on Europeans and common variants, this is an integrated study that **simultaneously analyzed common variants + rare coding + rare non-coding (CWAS) + CNV + STR from a single WGS dataset in a Korean cohort**.
- Discovery of novel loci not previously reported in European or East Asian studies, including **APCDD1, SAMD3, PTPRD**.
- Linked rare non-coding variants to cognitive impairment at the level of excitatory neuron–specific CREs (CWAS + Hi-C + single-cell).
- Analyzed structural variants such as STR expansions and CNV deletions in association with Aβ accumulation, and quantitatively presented a **cumulative effects model** of diverse genetic factors (incremental r²).
