# AnnQ: reference-based quantification of cellular abnormality at single-cell resolution

## One-Line Summary
A paper proposing AnnQ, a Python framework that reinterprets the "annotation uncertainty" produced but conventionally discarded by reference-based cell-type annotation tools — not as technical noise but as a biological signal of cell-identity instability — to continuously quantify the abnormality of each cell.

## Research Summary
In single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq), reference-based annotation tools (e.g., CellTypist, scArches, Azimuth) that transfer labels using large-scale atlases have become the standard. During an intermediate computational step, these tools generate a **probability distribution** over candidate cell types for each cell, but current workflows extract only the top label and discard the rest of the distributional information. Cells that show low confidence, competing label probabilities, or high entropy are usually treated as technical artifacts and filtered out or removed; however, the authors raise the concern that in perturbation experiments, it is precisely these cells that may represent the biologically meaningful identity deviations researchers are looking for.

Accordingly, the authors propose AnnQ (Annotation Quantification of cellular identity uncertainty), which repurposes annotation uncertainty as a **quantitative metric of cellular abnormality**. AnnQ extracts uncertainty-aware features (assurance/confidence, confidence gap, admixture ratio, normalized entropy) from the probabilistic cell-type assignment to build a multidimensional uncertainty space, and in this space computes an **out-of-reference (OOR) score** that measures how far each cell deviates from the reference population (normal, wild-type, control). At the same time, using threshold-based logic, it classifies cells into discrete groups: G0 (single identity / confident), G1 (ambiguous / low-confidence), G2 (multiple identities within the same lineage / transitional), and G3 (multiple identities across different lineages / abnormal).

The authors applied this framework to genetic-perturbation and drug-resistance datasets and showed that the OOR score detects abnormal and transitional cell states that are poorly resolved by conventional clustering or differential-abundance analyses. As a large-scale case, applying it to the Mouse Brain Perturbation Atlas (~1.94 million cells), they confirmed that perturbed populations show a systematic shift toward identity instability (expansion of G1 and G3 fractions), and found that perturbations of RNA processing/splicing (Hnrnpu, Eftud2) or chromatin remodeling (Smarcb1) induce the most severe identity instability.

In two in-depth case studies, they (1) quantified abnormal neuronal states that do not form distinct clusters in a Fezf2 perturbation model, and (2) tracked drug-tolerant (persister)–to–metastatic-precursor state transitions in longitudinal data from a stage IV breast cancer patient using annotation dynamics alone. Cross-atlas validation across five independent cancer cohorts demonstrated the reproducibility of known instability patterns and the novel discovery of multi-axis deviation structures.

In conclusion, AnnQ provides a perspective that is **complementary (orthogonal)** to existing approaches, measuring cell-identity deviation not in expression space but in annotation-probability space. It characterizes transitional and abnormal cell states — which discrete labels fail to capture — at single-cell resolution, and it is implemented in Python with source code and documentation publicly available.

## Methods & Technologies
- **Input data**: A probability matrix P generated by an external annotation tool (e.g., CellTypist), where P_ij is the probability that cell i belongs to cell type j (Σ_j P_ij = 1). Cell-level metadata (sample, genotype, cluster) are used for stratified analysis and visualization.
- **Uncertainty-aware features (4 types)**:
  - Assurance (P1): the maximum predicted probability, P1_i = max_j P_ij
  - Confidence gap (Delta): the difference between the top two probabilities, Δ_i = P1_i − P2_i (P2 is the second-largest probability)
  - Admixture ratio: A_i = P2_i / P1_i (relative strength of the second identity)
  - Normalized entropy: H_i = −(1/log K) Σ_j P_ij log P_ij (K = number of candidate cell types)
- **Cell-state classification (threshold-based)**: A hard threshold T_h (confident assignment) and an optional soft threshold T_s (weak but biologically meaningful signal). The number of cell-type "hits" exceeding the thresholds and their membership in the hierarchical structure (tissue/cell class) are evaluated to classify cells into G0–G3 as well as the soft groups G2s and G3s. In adaptive mode, thresholds are automatically suggested based on the IQR of the top-probability P1 distribution.
- **Reference distribution construction**: Using wild-type/control cells, the multivariate mean vector μ_c and covariance matrix Σ_c are estimated in a cluster-conditional manner. When a cluster has too few reference cells (n < 50), the method falls back to a global reference.
- **OOR score computation**: The squared Mahalanobis distance, OOR_i = (x_i − μ_c)^T Σ_k^{−1} (x_i − μ_c). This reflects the correlations among the uncertainty features and provides a scale-invariant deviation metric. For numerical stability, shrinkage regularization toward a diagonal covariance structure (coefficient λ = 0.1) is applied.
- **Cluster-level summary statistics**: median OOR shift (the difference in median OOR between conditions) and tail enrichment (the fraction of non-reference cells exceeding a given quantile of the reference OOR, e.g., the 90th percentile). The reference (wild-type) OOR distribution is treated as an empirical null model, and significance is assessed by a binomial test or permutation resampling, with multiple-testing correction via Benjamini–Hochberg FDR.
- **Implementation/pipeline**: Python 3.9+, providing both a CLI and a Python API. It takes probability matrices from tools such as CellTypist as input and outputs classification results, OOR scores, and summary statistics as CSV. YAML configuration files. Visualization (publication-ready plots for QC and result interpretation). MIT license.
- **Validation experiments**: (a) Semi-simulation — the Di Bella 2021 E15.5 cerebral cortex reference was systematically modified (reference-lineage dropout, sweeping reference coverage from 30% to 100%, progressive coarsening from 14 subtypes to 6 broad classes) to confirm that the G1 signal is not an artifact of reference sparsity. (b) Cross-reference reproducibility using 5 independent cerebral cortex atlases as alternative references. (c) Gene-level log-linear regression of the OOR score on expression to identify molecular drivers. Fisher's exact test to assess OOR-tail significance.
- **Applied datasets**: Mouse Brain Perturbation Atlas (~1,944,500 cells; 31 perturbations, 25 timepoints, 22 brain regions, 28 datasets; CellTypist Developing Mouse Brain / Whole Mouse Brain models), Fezf2 perturbation scRNA-seq, stage IV breast cancer patient longitudinal scRNA-seq (Pre/Post1/Post2/Meta), the Lambrechts 4-cancer cohort (lung, colorectal, ovarian, breast), and the PanCAF pan-cancer fibroblast atlas.

## Key Genes / Proteins
- **Fezf2**: Transcription factor determining the identity of subcerebral projection neurons (SCPN) in the cortex; its loss induces corticothalamic-like abnormal neuronal states (core of Case Study 1).
- **Hnrnpu**: Nuclear ribonucleoprotein essential for global RNA processing and splicing; upon perturbation shows the highest G1 fraction (72.4%) — the maximum identity instability.
- **Eftud2**: A spliceosome component; a high-uncertainty perturbation (G1 60.6%).
- **Smarcb1 / Smarca4**: Components of the SWI/SNF chromatin-remodeling complex (Smarcb1 G1 55.7%; Smarca4 shows low G1 but high G2, i.e., multiple identities within a lineage).
- **Ctnnb1 (β-catenin)**: Central Wnt-pathway effector; heterozygous deficiency yields low G1 (7.6%) and high G2 — maintaining multiple identities among closely related subtypes.
- **Hesx1**: Transcription factor; high G1 fraction (72.4%).
- **Satb2 / Slit2 / Sema3a**: Genes related to axon guidance and neuronal identity; significant molecular drivers of the elevated OOR in the Fezf2 CThPN population.
- **SNAI2 (Slug)**: Epithelial–mesenchymal transition (EMT) driver; upregulated in breast cancer G1 cells.
- **PDCD1, CXCL13, GZMB**: T-cell exhaustion markers; positive log2 fold change in the HighExh cohort.
- **HLA-A/B/C/D, CCL5, CXCR4**: Antigen-presentation and immune-interaction genes; upregulated along the lesion-associated axis (E19 cluster).
- **PRSS1, CPA1, CLPS, AMY2A**: Exocrine-like program; stronger shift in tumors.

## Main Findings
- **Core idea**: Using the full probability distribution of reference-based annotation (entropy, shape, multivariate structure) — instead of the top label — as a continuous quantitative metric of identity instability and cell deviation. No prior framework had directly exploited this.
- **Two complementary outputs**: (i) a discrete 4-group classification G0/G1/G2/G3 (confident single identity, ambiguous/low-confidence, multiple identities within a lineage, multiple identities across lineages, respectively) plus soft groups G2s/G3s; (ii) a continuous OOR score based on Mahalanobis distance.
- **Mouse Brain Perturbation Atlas (~1.94 million cells)**: Overall 54.9% G0, 28.1% G1, 13.2% G2/G2s, 3.8% G3/G3s. Perturbed populations shifted systematically toward identity instability relative to normal controls (e.g., expansion of G1 and G3/G3s fractions).
- RNA-processing (Hnrnpu, Eftud2) and chromatin-remodeling (Smarcb1) perturbations induced the most severe cell-identity instability. Ctnnb1 (Het) and Smarca4 maintained multiple identities within a lineage (G2) (Smarca4 G2 81.5%, the highest in the atlas).
- **Developmental-stage vulnerability**: Cells at early postnatal stages (P0–P7) showed extreme annotation uncertainty — G1 fractions of P0 62.8%, P1 76.1%, P4 90.4% (in contrast to 9.4% at the mature P28 stage). Interpreted as a biological signal of developmental plasticity.
- **Semi-simulation validation**: Removing a cell type from the reference caused even well-defined-identity cells to be captured as G1 (CThPN 99.9%, Interneurons 99.6%, SCPN 94.9%). Across the full reference-coverage range of 30%–100%, ΔG1 (Hom−Het) rose consistently by +12.2 to +18.2 pp → ruling out a reference-sparsity artifact. In 5 alternative cortical atlases, ΔG1 reproduced at +1.8 to +12.8 pp.
- **Case 1 (Fezf2)**: Standard clustering and differential-abundance analyses could not detect it because no distinct population is formed, but AnnQ detected an ~2.1-fold expansion of the ambiguous (G1) population (Hom 41.8% vs Het 20.4%). By cell type, G1 increases were SCPN +55.8 pp, CThPN +39.3 pp, DL CPN +20.0 pp, Migrating neurons +16.6 pp. The high OOR tail of Hom CThPN cells was significant (Fisher's exact, OOR>1: Het 11.1% vs Hom 46.9%, OR=7.05, P=1.14×10⁻¹⁰¹). Satb2, Slit2, and Sema3a were OOR drivers.
- **Case 2 (breast cancer)**: Because the reference lacks a "malignant" label, cancer cells were subject to forced classification into the "Luminal" lineage, causing OOR underestimation. AnnQ's uncertainty metrics uncovered hidden heterogeneity — before treatment, 63.2% of G1 cells expressed a weak DTP signature; after treatment, the G1 fraction expanded to 78.5% (EMT rising and the DTP program declining → transitioning to proliferative MPC). In the metastasis (Meta), CSC markers were reactivated and EMT decreased (MET). "Metastasis paradox": OOR is at its highest, yet the G0 fraction also increases — because metastatic cells converge onto a "LumSec-basal" reference state with intermediate luminal-basal features.
- **Cross-atlas (5 datasets)**: HighExh vs LowExh median OOR shift CRC 1.17, Breast 1.09, Ovarian 0.45, Lung 0.39. In PanCAF, the global G1 fraction increased Normal 0.7237 → Adjacent 0.7594 → Tumor 0.7814. The magnitude of the shift was non-monotonic across clusters — revealing parallel field-effect and tumor-core remodeling axes that discrete labels miss.

## Applications
- Detection and quantification of transitional and abnormal cell states that discrete labels miss in scRNA-seq analysis of perturbation/disease-progression contexts.
- Graded detection of coordinated identity instability that does not form distinct clusters in genetic-perturbation (e.g., CRISPR, knockout) screens.
- Tracking drug-tolerant persister → metastatic-precursor state transitions in cancer longitudinal studies using annotation dynamics alone.
- A discovery tool for validating the reproducibility of known instability patterns across multiple cohorts/atlases and systematically finding novel multi-axis deviation structures.
- Recovering biological signal from low-confidence cells that were discarded as QC filtering — integrating with expression-distance and compositional-abundance analyses to more completely capture perturbation-induced cellular changes.

## Extensibility / Future Directions
- Two intervention points allow study-specific context to be incorporated: a user-editable hierarchical JSON (defining custom label relationships and adding references) and the input probability-matrix CSV (merging known-annotation columns).
- The boundaries of "normal" uncertainty could be refined further with extended multimodal references and generative-modeling approaches.
- Because the absolute magnitude of the OOR is influenced by reference composition and lineage-specific variance structure, relative deviation metrics such as cluster-normalized OOR shift or within-lineage delta score can be used as complementary metrics for more context-aware interpretation.
- Reliability is highest when applied to well-curated reference atlases — improving reference quality drives performance.

## Disease Relevance
- **Cancer biology**: Quantifies pathological cell states that do not maintain canonical labels, such as drug-tolerant persister (DTP), metastatic precursor (MPC), cancer stem cell (CSC), and EMT/MET transition states. It compensates, via uncertainty metrics, for the problem where cancer cells are forced into normal-like categories because the reference lacks a malignant label. It detected T-cell exhaustion, tumor–adjacent field effects, and antigen-presentation activation axes across multiple cancer types.
- **Neurodevelopment/neurological disease**: Captures abnormal neuronal identity states created by transcription-factor loss — such as the collapse of SCPN identity upon Fezf2 loss — even without distinct clusters. Useful for interpreting the continuous, intermediate cell states that characterize disease progression, such as the stem-cell–differentiation continuum in colorectal cancer and metastatic heterogeneity in breast cancer.

## Novelty vs. Prior Work
- Existing annotation-confidence/uncertainty metrics have been used almost exclusively as **quality-control (QC) filters** to filter out low-confidence cells or to detect unassigned populations. AnnQ inverts this, being the first systematic framework to reinterpret uncertainty as a **biological quantitative signal**.
- Existing perturbation-quantification frameworks operate in expression space: (i) classifier-based cell-type separability (e.g., Augur), (ii) neighborhood-level compositional shift (e.g., Milo), (iii) mixture modeling of CRISPR signatures, and (iv) entropy-based potency (e.g., SCENT). AnnQ introduces an orthogonal information axis that **measures deviation in annotation-probability space rather than expression space** — so it can be integrated complementarily with existing methods.
- It detects coordinated identity instability that does not form distinct clusters at the **single-cell level** rather than the population level. Its Mahalanobis-distance-based, covariance-aware OOR score reflects the correlations among uncertainty features and provides scale-invariant deviation.
