# Five autism-associated transcriptional regulators target shared loci proximal to brain-expressed genes

- **PMID / DOI**: 38850535 / 10.1016/j.celrep.2024.114329

## One-Line Summary
A study showing that in the developing human and mouse cerebral cortex, five autism (ASD)-associated transcriptional regulators (ARID1B, BCL11A, FOXP1, TBR1, TCF7L2) co-bind shared loci near promoters within open chromatin; that the degree of this binding overlap strongly predicts the expression level of brain-expressed genes; and that CRISPRi-mediated repression of these regulators reproduces a convergent phenotype of neuronal loss and glial increase resembling that of ASD postmortem brain.

## Research Summary
Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) is a common, highly heritable neurodevelopmental disorder in which more than 100 genes have been implicated, primarily through rare loss-of-function variants. More than half of these ASD-associated genes encode transcriptional regulators (TRs), such as transcription factors (e.g., TBR1, FOXP1), histone-modifying enzymes (e.g., KMT5B), and chromatin remodelers (e.g., CHD8). The observation that damage to many different genes produces similar transcriptomic and phenotypic outcomes raises the possibility that these regulators converge on a common set of downstream targets/regulatory networks. However, heterogeneity in individual protocols, species, cell types, and developmental stages has made shared targets difficult to identify.

To address this, the authors selected five TRs (ARID1B, BCL11A, FOXP1, TBR1, TCF7L2) based on evidence of ASD association, expression during cortical development, evidence of direct DNA binding, and the availability of reliable antibodies, and performed ChIP-seq using an identical pipeline in human fetal frontal cortex (gestational week 23, GW23) and mouse cortex (embryonic days 15.5 and 18.5, E15.5/E18.5). They integrated and compared these data with histone ChIP-seq (H3K27ac, H3K4me1, H3K27me3, H3K4me3), publicly available ATAC-seq (open chromatin), and ENCODE ChIP-seq for 14 TRs from adult liver tissue.

As a key result, the five TRs showed substantial overlap in their binding sites, with overlap being particularly pronounced within open chromatin. Overlap at promoter regions (1–2,000 bp upstream of the transcription start site, TSS) was a very strong predictor of brain-expressed genes, and this signal was observed in 96 of 102 ASD-associated genes. Proximal loci bound by all five TRs and overlapping ATAC-seq open chromatin were defined as "5TRa"; the 12,224 proximal 5TRa sites span 26.2 Mbp and lie upstream of the TSS of 11,695 protein-coding and 4,739 non-coding transcripts. The number of ASD-associated TRs bound at a proximal site strongly and nonlinearly predicted the expression level of the corresponding gene (expression rose markedly when all five bound). Distal 5TRa sites were enriched for CTCF/BORIS motifs, suggesting involvement in promoter-enhancer and chromosomal loop formation.

For functional validation, lentiviral CRISPRi was introduced into P0 dCAS9-KRAB mouse cortical primary cultures to individually repress Arid1b and Tbr1. By day 8, NeuN+ neurons decreased approximately 3-fold and CALB+ cells approximately 6-fold, while GFAP+ astrocytes increased approximately 4.5-fold, and the RNA-seq transcriptomic signature correlated with the down/up-regulated modules observed in ASD patient postmortem brain (neuronal down, glial up). The authors further demonstrated that the VISTA enhancer hs399 (4TRa), located approximately 340 kbp downstream of the BCL11A TSS, is regulated by TBR1, and that its CRISPRi repression reduced BCL11A/Bcl11a expression approximately 10-fold. The authors concluded that functional convergence of the five ASD-associated TRs produces shared neurodevelopmental consequences to haploinsufficient damage — proposing a "clock mechanism" model in which failure of even one of many components impairs the function of the whole.

## Methods & Technologies
- **Transcriptional regulator ChIP-seq (TR ChIP-Seq)**: Targeting ARID1B, BCL11A, FOXP1, TBR1, TCF7L2. Human fetal frontal cortex (GW23) and mouse cortex (E15.5, E18.5). Antibodies from Santa Cruz (ARID1B/FOXP1/TBR1/TCF7L2) and Abcam (BCL11A). Antibody specificity was validated using a blocking peptide for FOXP1, and IgG or constitutive null controls for ARID1B/BCL11A/TCF7L2 and TBR1 as negative controls
- **Histone ChIP-seq**: H3K27ac, H3K4me1, H3K27me3, H3K4me3 (MNase-digestion-based). Human GW24, mouse cortex
- **ATAC-seq (reuse of public data)**: Used to define open chromatin in human GW18/19 cortex
- **ENCODE comparative analysis**: ChIP-seq for 14 TRs (e.g., CTCF, RAD21, SP1) from adult human liver tissue was reprocessed with the same pipeline to compare against expected overlap across heterogeneous tissues
- **Alignment/peak-calling pipeline**: BWA (v0.7.15) alignment (GRCh38/mm10), samtools (v1.10), MACS2 (v2.2.7.1) callpeak (q<0.01, fragment 200 bp), with IDR to confirm biological replicate quality
- **Annotation/overlap analysis**: Peak annotation based on GENCODE (human v31/hg38, mouse v23/mm10); BedTools intersect/closest for proximal (2,000 bp upstream of TSS)/distal classification and locus overlap computation
- **Coverage heatmaps**: deepTools (v3.5.1) computeMatrix/plotHeatmap
- **Motif analysis**: HOMER (v4.10.3) known/de novo motif enrichment, compared against genomic background and a control overlapping only ATAC-seq/ChIP-seq (nullTF)
- **Cross-species conservation**: UCSC PhastCons 100-way conservation, UCSC LiftOver (1% minimal overlap)
- **Brain-expression prediction**: Developing human cortex RNA-seq TPM (log2(TPM+1)) compared by promoter/distal 5TRa and ATAC-seq status (logistic regression)
- **VISTA enhancer validation**: Odds ratios (two-sided Fisher exact) for overlap between 1,942 VISTA enhancer regions and distal ≥3TRa peaks, computed across 20+ tissues
- **Distal TR–ASD gene interaction (ABC score)**: Activity-by-Contact model. Enhancer-gene predictions from GW18 ATAC-seq · H3K27ac ChIP-seq + GW17-18 Hi-C (10 kb resolution) (broadinstitute ABC-Enhancer-Gene-Prediction)
- **In vitro CRISPRi assay**: P0 dCAS9-KRAB mouse cortical primary cultures. sgRNAs targeting the Arid1b and Tbr1 TSS (2 kb upstream) delivered by lentivirus, with scrambled control. Guide design with CRISPOR, U6-stuffer-longTracer-GFP lentiviral vector. Analysis at days 2/4/8/12/16, with immunohistochemistry (NeuN, CALB, GFAP, caspase-3 markers)
- **qPCR**: SYBR Green, 7900HT Fast, DDCT method, normalized to Gapdh/eif1α housekeeping genes
- **RNA-seq (CRISPRi cells)**: Day 8; TPM computed with Salmon (v1.3.0), gene-level normalization/differential expression with tximport + DESeq2 (adjusted p≤0.05, |FC|≥2). Enrichment comparison with WGCNA ASD brain co-expression modules (e.g., M4/M16/M9/M19; HGNC ortholog conversion, Fisher exact + Bonferroni)
- **hs399-CT2IG enhancer transgenic mouse**: hs399 subcloned into Hsp68-CreERT2-IRES-GFP, FVB pronuclear injection
- **Single-cell transcriptomic cell-type enrichment**: Cell-type bias of genes near 5TRa assessed using human and mouse fetal cortex single-cell data
- **Statistics/software**: GraphPad Prism 7.0, ImageJ, Wilcoxon test, chi-square, permutation test, one-way ANOVA (Holm-Sidak/Tukey), Lillie test

## Key Genes / Proteins
- **ARID1B**: DNA-binding subunit of the BAF (SWI/SNF) chromatin-remodeling complex. Broadly expressed across tissues; ASD haploinsufficient risk gene
- **BCL11A**: DNA-binding subunit of the BAF (SWI/SNF) complex. In cortex, expression restricted to neurons
- **FOXP1**: Winged-helix transcription factor. Expressed in developing cortical and striatal neurons
- **TBR1**: T-box transcription factor. Expressed in deep-layer excitatory neurons of the developing cortex. Regulates the hs399 enhancer
- **TCF7L2**: HMG-Box transcription factor. Expressed in cortical/subcortical progenitors and thalamic excitatory neurons
- **CHD8, POGZ, KMT5B, SCN2A, SYNGAP1, SLC6A1, DYRK1A**: Representative ASD-associated genes (discussed as TR-binding targets or as non-TR examples)
- **hs399 (VISTA enhancer)**: Forebrain enhancer located approximately 340 kbp downstream of the BCL11A TSS. Bound by ARID1B, BCL11A, TBR1, and TCF7L2 (4TRa); regulates BCL11A expression
- **CTCF / BORIS (CTCFL), ZNF143 (Staf), THAP11 (Ronin)**: Factors for motifs enriched at distal/proximal 5TRa — related to chromosomal and promoter-enhancer looping

## Main Findings
- The number of ChIP-seq peaks for the five TRs ranged from 23,507 to 116,891. Reproducibility and conservation across species and assays were confirmed. BCL11A, FOXP1, TBR1, and TCF7L2 showed significant enrichment of known motifs at their peaks (ARID1B had no known motif)
- 32,962 proximal loci were targeted by one or more TRs. Of these, 18,190 (55.2%) were accompanied by ATAC-seq and 14,772 (44.8%) were not. 12,224 (37.5%) were targeted by all five TRs (5TR), and 99.0% of these overlapped open chromatin (64.6% of all proximal loci)
- In contrast, of 10,736 proximal loci targeted by only a single TR, only 8.2% were accompanied by ATAC-seq → multi-TR binding is strongly linked to open chromatin (permutation p<1×10⁻¹⁰)
- Of 107,653 distal independent loci, 3,219 (90.5% of distal 5TR) were accompanied by ATAC-seq. Distal 5TRa were enriched for CTCF/BORIS motifs
- Proximal 5TRa (12,224 loci): 26.2 Mbp, upstream of the TSS of 11,695 protein-coding + 4,739 non-coding transcripts. 67.4% (8,233 loci) were highly conserved across species (max PhastCons > 0.5)
- ASD-associated TR peaks were present proximally in 96 of 102 ASD genes (94.1%) (vs. 65.7% for non-ASD genes; χ² p=2×10⁻⁷). Proximal 5TRa loci = 36.2-fold enrichment (p=2×10⁻⁹)
- **Expression prediction**: Genes accompanied by proximal ATAC-seq peaks + ASD-associated TR peaks were strongly expressed. The more bound TRs at a proximal site, the more nonlinearly and markedly expression increased. Median log2(TPM+1) for the 102 ASD genes = 4.0 (high brain expression)
- Distal 5TRa (3,219 loci): 5.7 Mbp, near 850 protein-coding + 1,932 non-coding transcripts. 36 of 102 ASD genes (35.3%) had distal peaks (vs. 12.1% for non-ASD, χ²=50.5, p=1×10⁻¹²)
- **VISTA validation**: Of 998 elements active in E11.5 mouse, 53 overlapped 5TRa and 140 overlapped ≥3TRa. In the presence of distal ≥3TRa, the odds ratio for positive forebrain enhancers = 2.17 (FDR=5.5×10⁻⁵) and neural tube OR = 2.27
- **ABC**: 269 distal ≥3TRa loci were the nearest gene to 54 ASD genes, and 285 loci had ABC contact with 77 ASD genes. In GW17-18 cortex, 70 ASD genes (68.6%) had ≥3TRa detected within 100 kb, and 96 (94.1%) within 500 kb
- **CRISPRi functional validation**: Arid1b repression reduced expression to 75% by day 2 and 25% by day 8; Tbr1 to 50% by day 2 and 5% by day 8. Increased caspase-3 at day 2 (transient apoptosis). At day 8, NeuN+ decreased ~3-fold, CALB+ ~6-fold, and GFAP+ increased ~4.5-fold
- **Correlation with ASD postmortem brain**: Genes downregulated by Arid1b/Tbr1 CRISPRi were enriched in ASD-brain downregulated neuronal modules (M4, M16) (p≤7×10⁻⁹); upregulated genes were enriched in ASD-brain upregulated astrocyte/glial modules (M9, M19) (p≤9×10⁻¹²)
- **hs399 enhancer**: On a TBR1 null background, hs399 activity (GFP) was greatly reduced; CRISPRi repression of hs399 reduced Bcl11a RNA approximately 10-fold (Hbb and Drd1 controls were unchanged)
- **Cell types**: Genes near 5TRa were enriched in cortical progenitors and excitatory/inhibitory neurons. Cell-type deconvolution suggested an approximately 2.3% decrease in ASD upper-layer excitatory neurons

## Applications
- Provides a parsimonious mechanistic model for how the heterogeneous, pleiotropic functions of ASD risk genes converge on a common phenotype — a "clock mechanism in which failure of even one of many components impairs the whole"
- The open-chromatin promoter regions co-bound by the five TRs (5TRa) can be used as a strong predictive indicator of brain-expressed genes
- Distal regulatory elements (enhancers) near ASD genes can be nominated via ABC and VISTA, helping to narrow functional candidates for non-coding regulatory variants
- The CRISPRi-based primary-culture model reproduces the cell-type compositional changes of ASD postmortem brain (neurons↓, glia↑) → provides an in vitro experimental system for ASD convergent pathophysiology

## Extensibility / Future Directions
- Because the work is based on bulk cortical tissue, it cannot directly distinguish binding across cell types → extension to single-cell-level binding maps (single-cell ChIP/CUT&RUN, etc.) is needed
- Whether other ASD-associated transcriptional regulators beyond the five TRs follow the same promoter 5TRa pattern remains to be tested (authors' prediction)
- Whether damage to non-TR ASD genes (SCN2A, SYNGAP1, SLC6A1, etc.) converges on equivalent phenotypes remains unresolved
- Further validation is needed on how well CRISPRi's 75–95% repression reflects the 50% haploinsufficient loss in the developing human brain, and on whether the regulatory-complex model actually causes broad downregulation of highly expressed ASD genes
- The extent to which changes in neural-lineage cell-type composition contribute to ASD symptoms needs to be clarified

## Disease Relevance
This study presents a core pathophysiological mechanism of autism spectrum disorder (ASD) and neurodevelopmental disorders. That more than half of ASD-associated genes are transcriptional regulators, and that these converge on shared loci near the promoters of brain-expressed genes, supports transcriptional dysregulation as a major etiology of ASD. The combination of haploinsufficient damage and high neuronal expression of the target genes makes these genes particularly vulnerable to expression perturbation, resulting in the cell-type compositional changes of ASD postmortem brain — neuronal loss and astrocyte increase. The hs399–BCL11A case suggests that variants in non-coding regulatory elements near ASD genes may also contribute to risk.

## Novelty vs. Prior Work
- Unlike prior studies that examined the targets of individual ASD transcriptional regulators separately (CHD8, POGZ, TBR1, etc.), this is the first integrative resource to directly compare shared targets by performing parallel ChIP-seq of five TRs under an **identical protocol, homologous tissue, and identical developmental stage**
- By quantitatively comparing against the heterogeneous-tissue overlap of 14 adult liver TRs (ENCODE), it statistically demonstrated that overlap in the developing cortex exceeds expectation
- It newly established the nonlinear predictive relationship between the number of proximally bound TRs and expression level, and quantitative indicators showing that the 5TRa signature predicts brain-expressed and ASD genes
- Multi-layered integrative validation linking ChIP-seq (binding) → ATAC/H3K27ac/PhastCons (context) → ABC/VISTA (distal regulation) → CRISPRi/RNA-seq (function) → correlation with ASD postmortem-brain modules
