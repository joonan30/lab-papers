# Pan-cancer proteogenomic landscape of whole-genome doubling reveals putative therapeutic targets in various cancer types

## One-Line Summary
The first WGD proteogenomic study to integrate CPTAC pan-cancer multi-omics data (genome, transcriptome, proteome, phosphoproteome) across 1,060 samples, classifying whole-genome doubling (WGD) into three types based on genomic instability features, and — from transcription factor (TF) and kinase regulatory networks specifically activated in WGD tumors — proposing putative therapeutic targets such as BPTF and drug-repurposing candidates.

## Research Summary
Whole-genome doubling (WGD) occurs across diverse cancer types and plays a key role in tumorigenesis and genomic instability. However, the proteogenomic features and molecular regulators that govern WGD have remained undefined. To fill this gap, this study integrated large-scale multi-omics data to systematically characterize, for the first time, the molecular landscape of WGD tumors.

The authors integrated genome, transcriptome, proteome, and phosphoproteome data from the Clinical Proteomic Tumor Analysis Consortium (CPTAC). They detected WGD across 1,060 samples spanning 10 cancer types (BRCA, CCRCC, COAD, GBM, HGSC, HNSCC, LSCC, LUAD, PDAC, UCEC). WGD was present in 42% of all tumors (440/1,060), highest in HGSC (83%) and lowest in PDAC (9.4%). They then analyzed mutational signatures, molecular pathways, transcription factor regulation, and kinase phosphorylation networks to search for putative drug targets.

Centrally, the authors classified WGD into three types based on copy number (CN) signatures. **WGD type 1 (highly unstable genome)** appears in LSCC, LUAD, and HNSCC and was enriched for the CN7 (chromothripsis amplification) and CN15 (chromosomal LOH with 2x genome doubling) signatures. **WGD type 2 (focal instability)** was enriched for CN11 (focal LOH with 2x doubling) in BRCA and HGSC. **WGD type 3 (tetraploidy)** appears in CCRCC, COAD, GBM, PDAC, and UCEC and was enriched for CN2 (tetraploidy). This three-way classification forms the backbone of all subsequent analyses.

Functionally, activation of the cell cycle pathway and inactivation of the immune response pathway were observed specifically in WGD type 1 (revealing that a pattern previously reported as a feature of WGD overall is in fact confined to type 1). WGD type 2 showed activation of dTTP metabolism and DNA re-replication (endoreduplication) pathways, and WGD-positive COAD showed activation of Wnt signaling (presumed to be attributable to APC mutation). At the transcription factor level, the E2F family and MYC were activated in pan-cancer WGD (especially type 1), and — through a prioritization framework integrating protein expression, cancer dependency, and patient survival data — actual candidate targets were derived from among TFs previously considered "undruggable" (notably BPTF in HNSCC).

In conclusion, this study is the first proteogenomic analysis to characterize the molecular features of WGD at pan-cancer scale, presenting a comprehensive molecular network spanning from genomic regulation to kinase-phosphorylation activity and proposing BPTF as a target in WGD-positive HNSCC. In addition, through a kinase-based drug-repurposing network, it proposed drug candidates with selectivity for WGD such as palbociclib, erlotinib, and nintedanib. Validating the efficacy of drugs targeting WGD-specific TFs and kinases remains a future task.

## Methods & Technologies
- **Data source / cohort**: CPTAC pan-cancer multi-omics data. A total of 1,060 samples across 10 cancer types (per omics layer: BRCA 116, CCRCC 103, COAD 105, GBM 98, HGSC 78, HNSCC 108, LSCC 110, LUAD 110, PDAC 109, UCEC 94; see the pipeline schematic in Figure 1A).
- **Integrated omics layers**: Four-layer integration of Genome + Transcriptome + Proteome + Phosphoproteome.
- **WGD detection**: Computed WGD status (positive/negative) and WGD fraction (%) for each tumor. In the CPTAC cohort, the WGD fraction distribution showed a bimodal pattern (Figure 1B).
- **Mutational signature analysis**: Computed signature exposures for single-base substitutions (SBS), double-base substitutions (DBS), indels, and copy number alterations (CNA). Copy number signatures (CN signatures) were based on COSMIC (Catalogue of Somatic Mutations In Cancer). Key CN signatures: CN1 (diploidy), CN2 (tetraploidy), CN3 (octoploidy), CN7 (chromothripsis amp), CN9/10/11 (focal LOH, at 0x/1x/2x WGD respectively), CN15 (chromosomal LOH, 2x WGD), CN17 (HRD).
- **Dimensionality reduction / clustering**: UMAP (Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection) for sample clustering based on signature exposures (Figure 1E).
- **Differential expression analysis**: Differential gene and protein expression (DEG/DEP), comparing WGD-positive vs negative. Statistics used include Fisher's exact test (differentially mutated genes), Wilcoxon rank-sum test (TMB, cancer dependency), and paired t-test (cell viability), among others.
- **Gene set enrichment analysis (GSEA)**: Evaluated enrichment of pathways related to cell motility, immune response, cell cycle, and metabolism using the Normalised Enrichment Score (NES) (FDR < .05, log fold-change > .25).
- **Transcription factor activity estimation**: Computed TF scores from gene expression profiles. Integrated prioritization framework for WGD-active TFs (stepwise filtering): (1) significantly active TFs (TF score > 0 & FDR < 0.1, n=99) → (2) exclude TFs without protein expression data (n=43) → (3) select TFs with upregulated protein expression (DEP Log2FC > 0 & FDR < 0.05, n=9) → (4) select TFs with high cancer dependency in WGD (Wilcoxon p < 0.1, n=4) → (5) select TFs whose upregulated expression drives poor prognosis (p < 0.05, n=1 = BPTF/HNSCC).
- **Cancer dependency data**: Used cell viability after CRISPR gene knockout (DepMap-type data) to compare dependency in WGD-positive vs negative cell lines.
- **Patient survival analysis**: Kaplan-Meier curves (e.g., overall survival by high/low BPTF protein expression in HNSCC, p=0.017).
- **Kinase activity estimation**: Computed kinase scores from proteome and phosphoproteome profiles and identified kinases significantly upregulated in WGD (activity FDR < .1; protein expression upregulated FDR < .05 & log2FC > 0).
- **Drug repurposing network**: Built a kinase-drug-protein network targeting druggable kinases upregulated in WGD, evaluating selectivity via a normalised drug score.
- **Cell viability assays**: Functional validation of candidate targets by comparing viability in WGD-positive vs negative cell lines after siRNA knockdown (E2F3 in LUAD; SFPQ, REST, BPTF in HNSCC) (paired t-test).

## Key Genes / Proteins
- **TP53**: Tumor suppressor. Specifically enriched for mutation in WGD-positive BRCA, COAD, UCEC, and LUAD. Proposed as a major driver of WGD in COAD.
- **APC**: Tumor suppressor regulating the Wnt pathway. Mutation enhanced in WGD-positive COAD; presumed to activate Wnt signaling and drive WGD.
- **E2F family / E2F3**: Cell cycle transcription factors. Activated in pan-cancer WGD (especially type 1). E2F3 is a candidate target in WGD-positive LUAD (validated: reduced viability upon knockdown).
- **MYC**: Proliferation-related transcription factor. Activated in WGD tumors.
- **BPTF**: Chromatin-remodeling-related transcription factor. Key candidate therapeutic target in WGD-positive HNSCC (upregulated protein expression associated with poor survival; reduced viability upon knockdown).
- **GLI2**: Candidate target TF in LSCC WGD.
- **TFDP1 / TFDP2**: Candidate target TFs in LSCC, HNSCC, PDAC, and HGSC WGD (E2F partners).
- **SREBF2**: Candidate target TF in BRCA WGD (regulates lipid metabolism).
- **SFPQ / REST**: Candidate targets in WGD-positive HNSCC (reduced viability upon knockdown).
- **CDK1 / CDK2**: Cell cycle regulatory kinases. Activated in WGD across several cancer types; targeted by palbociclib and others.
- **CSNK2A1**: DNA-damage-response-related kinase; activated in WGD.
- **PAK4**: Kinase promoting proliferation and suppressing apoptosis; activated in WGD.
- **PRKAA2 / ULK1**: Kinases related to hypoxia-induced autophagy; activated in WGD.

## Main Findings
- **WGD prevalence**: WGD occurred in 42% of pan-cancer tumors (440/1,060 samples). Highest in HGSC (83%), lowest in PDAC (9.4%). The WGD fraction distribution in the CPTAC cohort was bimodal.
- **Clinical association**: No clinical phenotype was associated with WGD status at the pan-cancer level, but in HNSCC advanced stage was significantly associated with WGD (FDR < .05).
- **Three-way WGD classification (key finding)**: type 1 (highly unstable genome; LSCC, LUAD, HNSCC; CN7/CN15), type 2 (focal instability; BRCA, HGSC; CN11), type 3 (tetraploidy; CCRCC, COAD, GBM, PDAC, UCEC; CN2).
- **Driver mutations**: TP53 mutation was — contrary to prior assumptions — enriched not across all WGD but specifically in WGD-positive BRCA, COAD, UCEC, and LUAD (p < .05).
- **Amplification/expression**: In WGD type 1 (LSCC, LUAD, HNSCC), amplification of more than 80 genes led to upregulated gene and protein expression (cell cycle, metabolism). Type 1 had more than 100 enriched mutations, whereas other cancer types had fewer than 15 significant WGD-associated gene mutations.
- **Tumor mutational burden (TMB)**: WGD type 1 had higher TMB in WGD-positive tumors (p < .05). Conversely, COAD had lower TMB in WGD-positive tumors (p < .001) with enhanced TP53 and APC mutation (p < .05) → genomic instability is a catalyst of WGD in LSCC, LUAD, and HNSCC, while TP53 and APC mutation are the primary drivers of WGD in COAD.
- **Pathway specificity**: The pattern of cell cycle activation + immune response inactivation is specific to WGD type 1 (redefining what was previously known as a feature of WGD overall). WGD type 2 is enriched for dTTP metabolism and DNA endoreduplication pathways. WGD-positive COAD shows Wnt signaling activation (presumed attributable to APC mutation).
- **TF target prioritization**: An integrated framework narrowing 99 active TFs down to a final one (BPTF, HNSCC). BPTF is associated with poor prognosis in WGD-positive HNSCC (survival analysis p=0.017).
- **TF functional validation**: E2F3 knockdown (WGD-positive LUAD cell line) and BPTF/SFPQ/REST knockdown (WGD-positive HNSCC cell lines) produced a greater reduction in viability relative to WGD-negative counterparts.
- **Kinase activity**: Cancer-type-specific activity — CDK1/2 (cell cycle), CSNK2A1 (DNA damage response), PAK4 (proliferation, apoptosis suppression), PRKAA2/ULK1 (hypoxic autophagy). This suggests that WGD accelerates proliferation while inducing DNA damage and hypoxia, thereby requiring kinase activity to suppress apoptosis.
- **Drug repurposing candidates**: Drugs with WGD selectivity were proposed, including palbociclib (LSCC, BRCA), erlotinib/gefitinib/neratinib (LSCC), and nintedanib (PDAC).

## Applications
- **WGD-subtype-based precision medicine**: By stratifying WGD not as a single phenomenon but into three molecular subtypes, the study provides a basis for establishing cancer-type- and WGD-type-tailored treatment strategies.
- **Therapeutic target discovery**: Presents an integrated framework that prioritizes actually targetable candidates from among transcription factors previously regarded as "undruggable" (BPTF, E2F3, SREBF2, GLI2, TFDP1/2, etc.).
- **Drug repurposing**: Provides network-based rationale for re-applying already-approved/developed kinase inhibitors (palbociclib, erlotinib, nintedanib, etc.) to WGD-positive tumors.
- **Prognostic biomarker**: Potential for BPTF protein expression to serve as a prognostic biomarker in HNSCC.

## Extensibility / Future Directions
- **Drug efficacy validation**: Stated explicitly in the paper's conclusion — follow-up studies are needed to assess the efficacy of drugs targeting WGD-specific TFs and kinases.
- **Preclinical/clinical validation**: Cell-line-level knockdown validation (E2F3, BPTF, SFPQ, REST) can be extended to animal models and patient-derived models.
- **Cancer-type expansion**: Beyond the current 10 CPTAC cancer types, the generalizability of the three-way WGD classification framework can be tested in more cancer types and cohorts.
- **Reuse of the multi-omics integration framework**: The TF prioritization and kinase-drug network methodology can be extended to target discovery for genomic events other than WGD (e.g., specific CNAs, fusions).

## Disease Relevance
- WGD is a key event in tumor evolution and genomic instability. By revealing that WGD arises through distinct molecular mechanisms depending on cancer type (high genomic instability vs focal instability vs simple tetraploidization), this study deepens understanding of oncogenic mechanisms.
- The association of poor prognosis in WGD-positive HNSCC with BPTF suggests that WGD status can be used for prognostic stratification and targeted-therapy decisions.
- The finding that cell cycle activation and immune evasion (immune response inactivation) are specific to WGD type 1 (LSCC, LUAD, HNSCC) may provide a rationale for combination strategies of cell cycle inhibitors and immunotherapy in these cancer types.

## Novelty vs. Prior Work
- **First WGD proteogenomic analysis**: The authors state that this study is the "first proteogenomic analysis" to characterize the molecular features of WGD at pan-cancer scale. Whereas prior studies stayed mainly at the genome (copy number) level, this study integrated four layers — genome, transcriptome, proteome, and phosphoproteome.
- **Redefinition of WGD**: The study refined conventional wisdom by revealing that (1) TP53 mutation enrichment and (2) the pattern of cell cycle upregulation with immune response downregulation — previously reported as features of WGD overall — are in fact confined to specific WGD types/cancer types.
- **Proposal of a three-way classification system**: Classifying WGD not as a single phenomenon but into three CN-signature-based subtypes (type 1/2/3) is novel.
- **Targeting of undruggable TFs**: The framework that integrates protein expression, cancer dependency, and survival to derive candidate targets (e.g., BPTF) from TFs considered undruggable is novel.
