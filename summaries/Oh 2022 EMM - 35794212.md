# Augmentation of the RNA m6A reader signature is associated with poor survival by enhancing cell proliferation and EMT across cancer types

## Bibliographic Information
- **Authors**: Jaeik Oh (first author), Chanwoong Hwa (co-first author) et al.; Joon-Yong An (corresponding author), Sung-Yup Cho (corresponding author). (The two first authors, Jaeik Oh and Chanwoong Hwa, contributed equally; the two corresponding authors are An and Cho.)
- **Journal / Year**: Experimental & Molecular Medicine (EMM), 2022; 54:906–921 (published online July 6, 2022)
- **PMID / DOI**: 35794212 / 10.1038/s12276-022-00795-z
- **Article Type**: Original Article — an integrated multi-cancer transcriptome (bulk + single-cell) analysis with validation in an in-house gastric cancer cohort

## One-Line Summary
By analyzing the TCGA transcriptomes of 11 solid tumors together with an in-house gastric cancer cohort, this study shows that the augmentation of m6A reader proteins (especially IGF2BPs, the R2 signature)—rather than the m6A level itself (writer/eraser)—is a feature shared across cancer types, is associated with poor prognosis through cell-proliferation and EMT pathways, and thereby proposes m6A reader proteins as promising therapeutic targets.

## Research Summary
N6-methyladenosine (m6A) is the most abundant internal modification of eukaryotic mRNA and participates in post-transcriptional regulation, including splicing, transport, translation, stability, and degradation. m6A is dynamically regulated through the interplay of writers (methyltransferase complexes such as METTL3/METTL14), erasers (the demethylases ALKBH5 and FTO), and readers (proteins that directly bind m6A sites to carry out regulatory functions). Although alterations in m6A levels and m6A-related genes have been reported in many cancers, whether these are oncogenic or tumor-suppressive is inconsistent across cancer types, so the common mechanism of m6A alterations during carcinogenesis has remained unclear—the problem that motivated this study.

To identify the common features of m6A alterations across cancer types, the authors analyzed the transcriptomes of 11 solid tumors from the TCGA (and TARGET/GTEx) databases together with their own RNA-sequencing data from a Seoul National University Hospital gastric cancer cohort. Using the expression of 17 m6A-related genes, they derived writer (W), eraser (E), and reader (R) signatures, and further split the reader signature into an R1 signature based on YTHDF1/2/3 and an R2 signature based on IGF2BP1/2/3. As a surrogate index of the intracellular m6A level, they defined the log-scale difference between W and E (the W-E signature).

As a key result, although changes in the W and E signatures differed by cancer type, a strong positive correlation between W and E was observed in all cancer types (e.g., STAD Corr=0.90, COAD Corr=0.84), suggesting that writers and erasers offset each other to maintain homeostasis of the cellular m6A level. The prognostic effect of the W-E signature (i.e., the estimated m6A level) was inconsistent, being opposite across cancer types (in KIRC, LIHC, and UCEC a high W-E indicated poor prognosis, whereas in BLCA, BRCA, STAD, and LUSC a high W-E indicated favorable prognosis). This means that a simple increase or decrease in the m6A level is not consistently associated with prognosis.

In contrast, the reader signature—particularly the IGF2BPs-based R2 signature—was significantly increased in all 11 cancer types analyzed, and this increase was substantially attributable to copy number amplification of the reader genes. A high R2 signature was associated with significantly poor prognosis in most cancer types, and GSEA showed that it was strongly associated with enrichment of cell-cycle (G2/M checkpoint, E2F, MYC targets) and EMT-related gene sets. These findings were reproduced in the in-house gastric cancer cohort and in single-cell RNA-sequencing data, where the R2 signature showed a positive correlation with a proliferation marker (MKI67/Ki-67) and EMT genes.

In conclusion, the authors demonstrate that the common feature of m6A-related alterations during carcinogenesis is not the m6A level itself but the augmentation of m6A-binding proteins (readers), especially IGF2BPs, and propose that targeting m6A reader proteins is a promising strategy for cancer therapy.

## Methods & Technologies
- **Bulk RNA-seq datasets**: TCGA TARGET GTEx cohort (UCSC Xena, log2(TPM+0.0001)), 11 solid tumors — KIRC, GBM, STAD, BRCA, LUAD, BLCA, COAD, LIHC, LUSC, OV, UCEC.
- **Derivation of the 17-gene m6A signature**: 9 writers + 2 erasers + 6 readers. Each signature is the arithmetic mean of the expression of the corresponding genes. The W-E signature is W−E on the log scale (= the writer/eraser expression ratio), used as a surrogate index of the m6A level.
- **In-house gastric cancer cohort**: Seoul National University Hospital (2014–2017, IRB No. C-1402-054-555). 63 primary gastric cancer tissues + 40 matched normal tissues. TRIzol RNA extraction (RIN>5), TruSeq RNA Sample Prep Kit v2, Illumina HiSeq 2000 whole-transcriptome sequencing. Raw FASTQ aligned to GRCh37 with STAR, duplicates removed with Picard, quantified as FPKM. Data deposited in the ENA under accession **PRJEB40936**.
- **Copy number alteration (CNA) analysis**: TCGA PanCancer Atlas (cBioPortal), GISTIC2 discrete values (−2 homozygous deletion to +2 amplification). CNA–mRNA correlation by eta coefficient (values >0.3 shown). NCI Genomic Data Commons GISTIC2 classification (deletion/diploid/amplification).
- **Cancer cell line data**: DepMap (CCLE) mRNA and CNA. Pearson correlation (log2(RSEM+1)).
- **Survival analysis**: TCGA PANCAN (UCSC Xena). Cutoff optimization by maximally selected rank statistics with the maxstat package, Kaplan–Meier + log-rank test, CondMC method. survival and survminer packages. R 4.1.0.
- **Multivariate Cox regression**: covariates = sex, age, stage, and CIBERSORT-predicted immune-cell counts. coxph function, hazard ratios visualized with ggforest.
- **Validation cohorts (GEO microarray)**: GSE65194 (breast), GSE38832 (colon adeno), GSE7696 (GBM), GSE31210 (lung adeno), GSE157010 (lung squamous), GSE26193 (ovarian), GSE15459 (stomach adeno). Affy package RMA normalization, high/low split by the median, log-rank test.
- **Inflammation / premalignant-lesion data**: GSE55696 (stomach) — chronic gastritis, low-grade/high-grade gastric intraepithelial neoplasia (GIN), and early adenocarcinoma. Four-group one-way ANOVA.
- **Gene Set Enrichment Analysis (GSEA)**: MSigDB Hallmark gene sets, comparing W-E and R2 high/low groups. Normalized expression (log2(RPKM+1)). Heatmaps use −log10(P value), Euclidean distance.
- **Immune-cell profiling**: CIBERSORT / CIBERSORTx, LM22 matrix (22 immune cell subtypes), absolute mode. High/low group comparison by Student's t-test.
- **Single-cell RNA-seq (scRNA-seq)**: GSE180286 (breast), GSE148071 (NSCLC), GSE183904 (gastric), GSE159115 (renal, matched normal–tumor). Seurat (NormalizeData, log transformation), batch integration with Harmony, dimensionality reduction and clustering, annotation of tumor/stromal/immune cells by cell-type markers. Arithmetic mean of the m6A signatures computed, statistics with ggpubr and rstatix.
- **Statistical tools**: Pearson correlation, ANOVA, Student's t-test, pairwise Wilcoxon rank sum test (Benjamini–Hochberg correction), pairwise binomial test. R packages ggplot2, pheatmap, ggpubr, rstatix.

## Key Genes / Proteins
- **METTL3** — catalytic subunit of the m6A methyltransferase complex (writer); decreased expression in most cancer types.
- **METTL14** — component of the writer complex; increased expression in most cancer types (except BLCA, OV, UCEC).
- **METTL16, WTAP, KIAA1429 (VIRMA), RBM15, RBM15B, CBLL1, ZC3H13** — writer complex/accessory genes (9 writers in total); expression varies by cancer type.
- **ALKBH5, FTO** — m6A demethylases (erasers); remove m6A from mRNA to lower the overall m6A level; expression varies by cancer type.
- **YTHDF1** — m6A reader (R1); promotes mRNA translation.
- **YTHDF2** — m6A reader (R1); promotes mRNA degradation.
- **YTHDF3** — m6A reader (R1); participates in translation and degradation together with YTHDF1/2 (the three YTHDFs may be functionally redundant).
- **IGF2BP1 / IGF2BP2 / IGF2BP3** — insulin-like growth factor 2 mRNA-binding proteins, m6A readers (R2). Enhance mRNA stability and play oncogenic roles. IGF2BP1 and IGF2BP3 in particular are increased more than two-fold in most cancer types. Stabilize targets such as MYC, FSCN1, TK1, and MARCKSL1, and regulate metastasis. The central target of this study.
- **MKI67 (Ki-67)** — cell-proliferation marker; significant positive correlation with the R2 signature (gastric cancer R=0.333).
- **EMT markers (VIM, CDH2, FN1)** — indicators of epithelial–mesenchymal transition; negative correlation with the W-E signature, positive correlation with the R2 signature.

## Main Findings
- **W-E correlation**: In all 11 cancer types, the writer (W) and eraser (E) signatures are strongly positively correlated (e.g., STAD Corr=0.8998, COAD Corr=0.8424, all P<0.0001). This is also reproduced in CCLE cell lines except for LIHC → writers/erasers offset each other to maintain cellular m6A homeostasis.
- **Individual gene expression**: METTL3 is decreased in most cancer types (prominently in BRCA, LUAD, BLCA, LUSC, OV, UCEC, etc.), whereas METTL14 is increased in most. IGF2BP1 and IGF2BP3 are increased more than two-fold in most cancer types. KIRC, GBM, STAD, and BRCA show overall increases in W, E, and R expression.
- **Universal increase of the R2 signature**: The IGF2BPs (R2) signature is significantly increased relative to normal in **all 11 cancer types** analyzed (P<0.05). The YTHDFs (R1) are also increased in most cancer types.
- **Copy number alterations**: In all 11 cancers, amplification is more frequent than deletion in the reader genes (especially YTHDF1, YTHDF3, IGF2BP2, IGF2BP3). The proportion of reader amplification is high in LUSC, OV, LUAD, and BRCA. CNA and mRNA expression have a positive linear relationship → the reader increase is partly attributable to copy number amplification. In contrast, writers and erasers show more deletion than amplification (except KIAA1429 and CBLL1).
- **The prognostic effect of W-E (estimated m6A level) is opposite by cancer type**: High W-E is poor prognosis — KIRC (p=0.034), LIHC (p=0.00087), UCEC (p=0.019), with LUAD and OV showing only a trend. High W-E is favorable prognosis — BLCA (p=0.00031), BRCA (p=0.033), STAD (p=0.017), LUSC (p=0.023), with COAD and GBM showing only a trend. → A simple increase or decrease in the m6A level is not consistently associated with prognosis.
- **W-E GSEA (2 clusters)**: Cluster 1 (GBM, UCEC, KIRC, LIHC) shows poor survival with high W-E; Cluster 2 (COAD, STAD, LUAD, LUSC, BRCA, BLCA, OV) shows the opposite. Cell-cycle-related sets (G2/M checkpoint, mitotic spindle, E2F, MYC targets) are enriched in high W-E in both clusters. In Cluster 2, the low-W-E group shows enrichment of EMT and TGF-β signaling (associated with poor prognosis). EMT markers (VIM, CDH2, FN1) are negatively correlated with W-E in all Cluster 2 cancer types.
- **Immune cells (W-E)**: In Cluster 2, low-W-E tumors are enriched for M2 macrophages, mast cells, and dendritic cells (poor prognosis), whereas in Cluster 1 high W-E is enriched for eosinophils and M0 macrophages.
- **High R2 = poor prognosis**: High R2 is significantly associated with poor survival in BLCA (p=0.012), BRCA (p=0.04), COAD (p=0.0061), KIRC (p<0.0001), LIHC (p=0.0076), LUAD (p<0.0001), STAD (p=0.035), and UCEC (p=0.028). Only LUSC is opposite (high R2 favorable). This is reproduced in the validation cohorts (BLCA, BRCA, COAD, LUAD, STAD, OV) and remains significant after multivariate adjustment (for sex, age, stage, and immune cells).
- **R2 GSEA**: The high-R2 group shows common enrichment of G2/M checkpoint, E2F targets, and MYC targets (cell cycle / oncogenesis). EMT-related sets (hypoxia, apical junction, Wnt beta catenin) are enriched. Estrogen response (early/late) is negatively correlated with R2 in BRCA, UCEC, and OV (suggesting possible loss of hormone receptors and association with triple-negative breast cancer). Immune gene sets (IL2-STAT5, IL6-JAK-STAT3, inflammatory response) are heterogeneous by cancer type (enriched in BRCA, BLCA, KIRC; depleted in LUSC, LUAD, UCEC, OV) — partly consistent with differences in immune-cell infiltration.
- **Gastric cancer cohort validation**: 63 tumor + 40 normal. Positive W-E correlation (R=0.645, P<0.0001), homogeneous and significant increase of R2. R2 is significantly correlated with MKI67 (R=0.333, P<0.0001) and positively correlated with the EMT signature (mean of CDH2, FN1, VIM) (R=0.17, P=0.082). The high-W-E group tends toward MYC target enrichment (NES=1.60, P=0.079), and the low-W-E group shows significant EMT enrichment (NES=−1.93, P<0.0001).
- **Single-cell analysis**: Although most m6A-related genes have a higher fraction of expressing cells in non-tumor cells, the m6A signature (sum) value is dominant in tumor cells. W, E, and R1 are mostly derived from tumor cells in breast and lung cancer, whereas in gastric cancer R2 is derived mainly from immune cells. In matched normal–tumor renal cancer, E, R1, and R2 are significantly increased in the tumor epithelium (consistent with bulk KIRC). Cancer cells account for the largest proportion of the overall m6A signature. MKI67 and the R2 signature are significantly positively correlated in tumor cells (breast P=2.4e-4; lung and gastric P<2.2e-16), and EMT genes are also positively correlated with R2 in tumor cells (breast P=7.1e-4; lung P=1.6e-11).
- **Premalignant lesions (stomach)**: R1 is significantly increased in high-grade gastric intraepithelial neoplasia (HGIN), and R2 is significantly increased in HGIN and early gastric cancer → the reader increase occurs from the early stages of carcinogenesis.

## Applications
- **Prognostic biomarker**: The R2 (IGF2BPs) signature can be used as a poor-prognosis predictor in various solid tumors (an independent risk factor even after multivariate adjustment). It provides particularly strong prognostic separation in KIRC, LUAD, and others.
- **Therapeutic target**: Provides a rationale for therapeutic strategies targeting m6A reader proteins, especially IGF2BPs. It cites and reconfirms that IGF2BP inhibition (knockdown/knockout, small-molecule inhibitors) reduces proliferation, migration, and invasion.
- **Pan-cancer perspective**: Moving beyond the debate over the opposite roles of m6A writers/erasers across cancer types, it proposes the increase of readers (especially R2) as a common feature, shifting the focus of future m6A research toward reader proteins.
- **Hormone-receptor-related cancers**: In breast and gynecologic cancers, the negative correlation between R2 and estrogen response can be applied to understanding hormone-responsive/triple-negative subtypes.

## Extensibility / Future Directions
- Whether the W-E signature (writer/eraser ratio) truly represents the actual cellular m6A level could not be verified because TCGA lacks m6A quantification data → the correlation needs to be validated by experimental m6A measurement (e.g., m6A-seq/MeRIP).
- Because individual erasers such as FTO/ALKBH5 act in a site-specific manner on particular targets, the single W-E index has the limitation of not representing individual m6A states → precise gene- and target-level analysis is needed.
- The possible functional redundancy of YTHDF1–3 was handled with an integrated R1 signature, but the unique roles of individual YTHDFs need to be elucidated.
- The molecular mechanisms of the tumor-type-specific effects of the m6A level on EMT and immune-cell infiltration need further elucidation (e.g., myeloid-cell m6A and M2 macrophages).
- This work can be extended to preclinical and clinical development of IGF2BPs-targeting small molecules/inhibitors.

## Disease Relevance
- **Cancer (pan-cancer)**: In 11 solid tumors (clear-cell renal cell carcinoma, glioblastoma, gastric adenocarcinoma, breast cancer, lung adeno/squamous cell carcinoma, bladder urothelial carcinoma, colon cancer, hepatocellular carcinoma, ovarian cancer, endometrial cancer), m6A dysregulation is involved in carcinogenesis and prognosis. The increase of readers (R2/IGF2BPs) promotes cell proliferation (G2/M, E2F, MYC) and EMT, associating with tumor progression, metastasis, and poor survival.
- **Gastric cancer**: In the in-house cohort, premalignant lesions (GIN), and single-cell data, the association of R2 increase with proliferation and EMT is consistently confirmed, and the reader increase is involved from the early stages of gastric carcinogenesis.
- **Breast/gynecologic cancers**: R2 increase is associated with reduced estrogen/progesterone receptor expression and may be related to poor-prognosis subtypes such as triple-negative breast cancer and high-grade ovarian cancer.

## Novelty vs. Prior Work
- Prior studies reported that the oncogenic versus tumor-suppressive roles of m6A writers/erasers (METTL3, METTL14, FTO, etc.) are opposite by cancer type; this study is novel in **integrating 11 solid tumors to identify a common feature across cancer types**.
- It presents the integrative conclusion that the increase of **m6A-binding proteins (readers), especially IGF2BPs (R2)**—rather than the m6A "level" itself—is more decisive for carcinogenesis and prognosis.
- A signature-based approach that estimates the cellular m6A level from the writer/eraser ratio (W-E) and subdivides readers into R1 (YTHDFs) and R2 (IGF2BPs).
- Multilayered integrative validation spanning **bulk RNA-seq (TCGA + in-house gastric cancer cohort) + copy number alterations + multiple GEO validations + CIBERSORT immune profiling + single-cell RNA-seq + premalignant lesions**.
- It shows via CNA–expression correlation that the reader increase is partly attributable to copy number amplification.
