# Proteogenomic analysis reveals non-small cell lung cancer subtypes predicting chromosome instability, and tumor microenvironment

## Bibliographic Information
- **Authors**: Kyu Jin Song, Seunghyuk Choi, Kwoneel Kim, Hee Sang Hwang (co-first authors, 4 people) and others. Seungjin Na, Se Jin Jang, **Joon-Yong An**, and Kwang Pyo Kim jointly supervised the study. **Corresponding author: Kwang Pyo Kim (kimkp@khu.ac.kr, Kyung Hee University)**
- **Journal / Year**: Nature Communications, 2024, 15:10164 (Received 2023-11-02, Accepted 2024-11-06, Published online 2024-11-23)
- **PMID / DOI**: 39580524 / 10.1038/s41467-024-54434-4
- **Article Type**: Original research (proteogenomic multi-omics resource / classification study)

## One-Line Summary
An integrated multi-omics analysis (genome, transcriptome, proteome, phosphoproteome, and acetylome) of a Korean cohort of 229 non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) patients that defines five molecular subtypes beyond histology (Metabolic, Alveolar-like, Proliferative, Hypoxic, and Immunogenic) and reveals how each subtype connects to whole-genome doubling (WGD), chromosome instability, the tumor microenvironment, metastasis, and adjuvant therapy response.

## Research Summary
Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) is histologically classified into lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD) and lung squamous cell carcinoma (LSCC), but some tumors are histologically ambiguous, and other pathophysiological features or microenvironmental factors may be more prominent. Prior proteogenomic studies mostly focused on a single subtype, either LUAD or LSCC, and excluded histologically ambiguous cases, limiting how well they represented the actual NSCLC patient population. In addition, existing prognostic factors alone give inaccurate predictions of postoperative survival, and neither a molecular basis nor a consensus biomarker for predicting response to adjuvant chemotherapy has been established.

This study generated genome (WES), transcriptome (RNA-seq), and proteome / phosphoproteome / acetylproteome (TMT-based) data for 229 Korean NSCLC patients who underwent surgery at Asan Medical Center between 2010 and 2019 (discovery cohort), and integrated 462 patients from prior multi-omics studies (LUAD from Gillette and Xu, LSCC from Satpathy, and NSCLC from Lehtio) as a replication cohort to broaden the range of histologic subtypes and to identify molecular subtypes with prognostic and therapeutic implications. The discovery cohort consisted of 139 LUAD (61%), 63 LSCC (27%), and 27 other (12%) cases, and 137 patients were preferentially selected for lymph node metastasis positivity.

Using non-negative matrix factorization (NMF) clustering to integrate the protein, phosphorylation, and acetylation data, the study defined five molecular subtypes. **Subtype 1 (Metabolic, n=55)** is dominated by female LUAD patients and is characterized by EGFR and TP53 mutations, a high frequency of WGD, and oxidative phosphorylation / mitochondrial pathways. **Subtype 2 (Alveolar-like, n=45)** is LUAD with frequent EGFR mutations, a stable oncogene-driven subtype without WGD, with activated IL-33 and Notch pathways suggesting early tumorigenesis and early stage. **Subtype 3 (Proliferative, n=52)** is strongly associated with WGD (OR 13.5), PIK3CA mutation, LSCC, males, and smokers, and is a proliferative, tumor-suppressor-deficient subtype with chromosome instability in which cell-cycle pathways such as E2F/MYC, G2M, and CDK are extremely upregulated. **Subtype 4 (Hypoxic, n=43)** is independent of any particular histology but is significantly associated with metastasis (OR 3.0); it is a chromosomally stable, mesenchymal subtype with upregulated hypoxia, PI3K-Akt, and neutrophil degranulation pathways, and has the worst prognosis. **Subtype 5 (Immunogenic, n=34)** is a hot tumor with high tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes (TILs) and an upregulated TNFα-NF-κB pathway, representing a chromosomally stable, tumor-suppressor-driven inflammatory subtype.

Key results were: (1) WGD arises through different drivers and pathways in LUAD (Subtype 1) versus LSCC (Subtype 3), and the targeted inhibitor selinexor showed efficacy against XPO1 overexpression in Subtype 3 in WGD-positive LSCC organoids. (2) Subtype 4 is characterized by poor prognosis and frequent metastasis, and SLK (S347) and LRRFIP1 (S581) phosphorylation were reproduced as poor-prognosis markers. (3) The tumor immune microenvironment (TIME) is divided into three immune clusters, hot-tumor-enriched (HTE), cold-tumor-enriched (CTE), and NAT-enriched, and SMARCA4 mutation was associated with expression of the immunomodulator SLAMF7 and the HTE state. (4) Neoantigens and non-canonical cryptic MAPs (MHC class I-binding peptides) were predicted, and recurrent cryptic MAPs were significantly associated with improved survival. (5) Patients with the immunogenic Subtype 5 showed a clear survival benefit from adjuvant chemotherapy / chemoradiotherapy, whereas the driver-oncogene-rich Subtypes 1 and 2 showed no benefit.

In conclusion, this study presents a map of NSCLC molecular subtypes that transcends histology, and by linking WGD, chromosome instability, metastasis, the immune microenvironment, and adjuvant therapy response to each subtype, it expands the evidence base for precision medicine and individualized treatment.

## Methods & Technologies
- **Cohort**: 229 Korean NSCLC patients (Asan Medical Center, surgery 2010-2019; IRB 2019-1210), with matched NAT (normal adjacent tissue) tumors. Discovery cohort of 229 patients plus a replication / integration cohort of 462 patients (CPTAC series, etc.); the integrated NMF used 447 patients (combined NMF).
- **Whole-exome sequencing (WES)**: SureSelect V6-Post, Illumina NovaSeq, tumor ~300X / NAT ~100X. 33,301 somatic small variants (mean 145/sample), 470,836 CNAs, mean TMB 2.7. GATK4 Mutect2 (somatic variants) / HaplotypeCaller (germline), BWA-MEM alignment, Funcotator annotation.
- **CNV/WGD detection**: CNVkit (v0.9.8), FACETS (v0.16.0). WGD was defined as an absolute copy number ≥2 on autosomes over more than 50% of the genome. Copy-number signature NMF (rank 7) with COSMIC signature v3 plus the R package Sigminer (v2.1.5).
- **RNA-seq**: Total RNA (Ribo-Zero) library, NovaSeq6000, mean 200 million reads/sample (~120M deep coverage). 205 tumors + 85 NAT. STAR alignment, Salmon quantification, DESeq2 normalization. 20,088 of 60,688 transcripts selected.
- **Isoform / splicing**: StringTie, IsoformSwitchAnalyzeR (+ DRIMseq); novel isoforms annotated with CPAT, PFAM, SignalP, and IUPred2A.
- **Fusion genes**: STAR + Arriba (v1.2.0), with normal-tissue blacklist filtering.
- **Proteome / PTM**: TMTpro 16-plex isobaric labeling, common reference pool (CR), mid-pH RP fractionation, Q Exactive HF-X. Phosphopeptides enriched by IMAC (Ni-NTA), acetylpeptides enriched by Acetyl-Lysine motif (PTMScan). 10,788 proteins / 40,738 phosphosites / 5,975 acetylation sites (quantified in ≥30% of samples).
- **Subtype classification**: Non-negative matrix factorization (NMF) integrating protein, phosphorylation, and acetylation data → five subtypes. Pathway enrichment by GSVA / PTM-SEA.
- **Kinase activity**: decoupleR (MLM-based), PHONEMeS prior-knowledge network.
- **Neoantigen / cryptic MAP prediction**: MHCflurry 2.0, NetMHCpan-4.1, OptiType (HLA typing), Comet (MS search). Canonical neoantigens distinguished from non-canonical cryptic MAPs and confirmed by MS.
- **Single-cell / immune**: scRNA-seq atlas (luca, local.icbi), xCell (enrichment of 64 cell types), the CancerSubtypes package, ARACNe / VIPER (protein regulatory activity), GSVA.
- **Survival / regression**: Kaplan-Meier, log-rank (Mantel-Cox), Cox proportional hazards (CoxPHFitter, MASS, GLM). Fisher's exact test, Wilcoxon rank-sum, Kruskal-Wallis.
- **Organoid / drug**: SG Medical-derived lung tumoroids, selinexor (XPO1 inhibitor) treated at 8 concentrations, viability / IC50 measured by CellTiter-Glo 3D.

## Key Genes / Proteins
- **EGFR**: Mutated / pathway-upregulated in the LUAD-lineage Subtypes 1 and 2; Subtype 1 features in-frame deletions and copy-number gain.
- **TP53**: Tumor-suppressor mutation axis of Subtypes 1 and 3; co-occurs with SOX2 amplification in Subtype 3.
- **PIK3CA**: Significantly enriched for mutations in Subtype 3.
- **SOX2**: Central to the 3q amplification region of Subtype 3; co-occurs with TP53 mutation.
- **XPO1 (CRM1)**: Overexpressed in Subtype 3 (K693 protein acetylation); selinexor target. Also upregulated together with the splicing factors SRSF1 (S199/K179) and SRSF2 (T25/S26).
- **SLK (Ste20-like kinase)**: S347 phosphorylation in Subtype 4 (by CSNK2A1); poor-prognosis marker involved in tumor progression.
- **LRRFIP1**: S581 phosphorylation in Subtype 4; promotes epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT) via Wnt/β-catenin pathway regulation; poor prognosis.
- **CSNK2A1 / GSK3B**: Two kinases significantly upregulated in Subtype 4 (PI3K-AKT pathway phosphorylation).
- **KRAS**: Enriched for mutations in the immunogenic Subtype 5 (OR 4.51).
- **SMARCA4**: Positively correlated with expression of the immunomodulator SLAMF7 and associated with the HTE (hot tumor) state.
- **SLAMF7**: Immunomodulator associated with SMARCA4 mutation; candidate immunotherapy target.
- **RPS6KA3, CDK1/CDK2/CDK4, CDK12, PIK3C3**: Kinases upregulated in Subtype 1, related to accelerated cell cycle, DNA damage response, and autophagy.
- **ANGPT2, VEGF, TIMP3, CDKN1B**: Subtype 4 hypoxia / angiogenesis pathway and prognostic factors (CDKN1B downregulation = progressive phenotype).

## Main Findings
- Integrated protein / phosphorylation / acetylation NMF defined **five molecular subtypes**: Subtype 1 Metabolic (n=55, 24.0%), 2 Alveolar-like (n=45, 19.7%), 3 Proliferative (n=52, 22.7%), 4 Hypoxic (n=43, 18.8%), 5 Immunogenic (n=34, 14.9%). The phosphoproteome contributed 80% of the subtype-identifying features (911/1,134 features).
- **Subtype 3 (Proliferative)** is strongly associated with WGD (OR 13.5, P=5×10⁻¹⁰) and PIK3CA mutation (OR 3.89); 65% LSCC, 83% male, 85% smokers. E2F/MYC (adj P=4.7×10⁻²²), G2M, and CDK pathways are extremely upregulated.
- **Subtype 4 (Hypoxic)** is associated with metastasis (OR 3.0, P=5.6×10⁻³) and has the highest metastasis rate, with the worst prognosis among the five subtypes (P=8.4×10⁻³). SLK (S347) and LRRFIP1 (S581) phosphorylation were reproducibly associated with poor survival in both the discovery cohort and the CPTAC cohort (SLK S347 P=3.0×10⁻⁶).
- **Histology-specific nature of WGD**: In LUAD, WGD is activated in Subtype 1 (51% WGD, mediated by EGFR and tumor-suppressor genes); in LSCC, in Subtype 3 (64% WGD, mediated by 3q amplification and TP53). Enriched for CN signature CN16 (Subtype 1) and CN10/CN16 (Subtype 3).
- **XPO1 targeted-therapy potential**: Selinexor showed higher drug sensitivity in WGD-positive LSCC organoids than in other samples.
- **Immune microenvironment**: Three immune clusters, HTE / CTE / NAT-enriched. HTE is enriched for CD8+/CD4+ T, B, NK, neutrophils, DCs, monocytes, and macrophages, with good prognosis. Tregs are enriched in HTE but are immunosuppressive and associated with poor survival.
- **SMARCA4-SLAMF7 axis**: SMARCA4 mutation positively correlates with SLAMF7 RNA / protein expression and HTE (in both the discovery cohort and an independent cohort). VEGF-A negatively correlates with TILs.
- **Neoantigen / cryptic MAP**: 85,430 neoantigen candidates and 775 cryptic MAPs predicted. Non-coding / non-annotated transcripts account for 90.97% of the origin of cryptic MAPs. Confirmed cryptic MAPs and recurrent cryptic MAPs (12 confirmed) were significantly associated with improved survival (HR, P=0.03).
- **Adjuvant therapy response**: The immunogenic Subtype 5 showed a clear survival benefit from adjuvant chemotherapy / chemoradiotherapy (RFS P=0.0016). The driver-oncogene-rich Subtypes 1 and 2 showed no benefit.

## Applications
- Provides a **molecular subtype classification system** that represents the actual NSCLC patient population, including histologically ambiguous cases → patient stratification and prognosis prediction.
- **Subtype-specific targeted-therapy guidance**: XPO1 inhibitor (selinexor) for the WGD-positive proliferative subtype (Subtype 3); SLK / LRRFIP1 / PI3K-Akt targeting for the metastatic hypoxic subtype (Subtype 4); adjuvant chemotherapy / immune checkpoint inhibitors for the immunogenic subtype (Subtype 5).
- **Immunotherapy biomarkers**: Use SMARCA4 mutation, SLAMF7, and the HTE state to predict immunotherapy response and as targets.
- **Basis for cancer vaccine development**: Recurrent cryptic MAPs validated by immunopeptidomics are proposed as tumor-specific antigen vaccine candidates.
- Selecting patients expected to benefit from adjuvant chemotherapy (Subtype 5) to avoid unnecessary cytotoxic treatment.

## Extensibility / Future Directions
- Replace WES-based WGD estimation with **whole-genome sequencing (WGS)** to improve accuracy.
- Investigate possible **ethnic differences** in specific subtypes such as Subtype 4 (hypoxic / metastatic) (requires a larger normal-sample cohort).
- Strengthen proteomic (immunopeptidomics) validation of cryptic MAPs by expanding the normal-sample cohort.
- **Prospective cohort studies** are needed to validate the subtype-specific effects of targeted drugs such as selinexor.
- Comprehensive studies elucidating the effects of adjuvant therapy and tumor-microenvironment-targeted therapy across the various subtypes.

## Disease Relevance
- By elucidating molecular heterogeneity that transcends the histologic classification of NSCLC (LUAD and LSCC), the study presents subtype-specific molecular mechanisms of poor prognosis, metastasis, chromosome instability, and immune evasion.
- Identification of the metastatic / poor-prognosis subtype (Subtype 4) and its prognostic markers (SLK S347, LRRFIP1 S581) contributes to discovering targets for suppressing lung cancer metastasis.
- The immunogenic subtype, SMARCA4 mutation, and the SLAMF7 axis suggest patient selection for lung cancer immunotherapy and novel immunomodulatory targets.

## Novelty vs. Prior Work
- Whereas prior proteogenomic studies were confined to a single subtype (LUAD or LSCC), this study performs an integrated analysis on a **mixed NSCLC cohort** that includes histologically ambiguous cases.
- Defines five subtypes via multi-omics NMF integrating protein, phosphorylation, and **also the acetylproteome** (phosphorylation contributes 80% of the classification).
- First to define a novel NSCLC subtype (Subtype 4, mesenchymal hypoxic type) associated with metastasis and poor prognosis independent of histologic type.
- Demonstrates proteogenomically that WGD arises through **different drivers and pathways** in LUAD versus LSCC.
- Predicts and confirms by MS **cryptic MAPs** derived from non-coding / non-annotated transcripts, links them to prognosis and immunity, and proposes them as vaccine candidates.
- Validates the reproducibility of the subtype classification using an integrated cohort of 462 CPTAC-series patients.
