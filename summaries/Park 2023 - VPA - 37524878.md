# Dysregulation of the Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway via Rnf146 upregulation in a VPA-induced mouse model of autism spectrum disorder

## One-Line Summary
By quantitatively profiling the prefrontal cortex (PFC) proteome of autistic mice exposed to valproic acid (VPA) in utero, this study identified upregulation of the E3 ubiquitin ligase **Rnf146** as a hub that activates the Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway, and demonstrated that overexpressing Rnf146 in the adult PFC reproduces social deficits together with increased excitatory synaptic transmission.

## Research Summary
Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by impaired social interaction and communication, repetitive behaviors, and restricted interests. Beyond genetic factors, environmental factors such as prenatal drug exposure also contribute to its pathogenesis. Valproic acid (VPA; 2-propylpentanoic acid) is a short-chain fatty acid used clinically as a mood stabilizer and antiepileptic, but prenatal exposure is known to cause neural tube defects and autism-like behavioral and developmental abnormalities. Consequently, rodents exposed to VPA in utero are a representative environmental model of ASD. However, it has remained unclear how such prenatal factors induce behavioral deficits at the adult stage, and in particular, the molecular-level changes in the prefrontal cortex (PFC), which governs social behavior, were poorly defined.

This study aimed to identify differentially expressed proteins (DEPs) and altered signaling pathways in the PFC of VPA-exposed mice using high-resolution mass spectrometry-based quantitative proteomics. First, using the three-chamber test, the authors confirmed that VPA-exposed mice showed clear deficits in both social preference and social recognition (significantly reduced preference index). They then quantified the PFC proteome by TMT-based high-throughput quantitative proteomics (Orbitrap), quantifying a total of 5,624 proteins, of which 941 were defined as VPA-DEPs (460 upregulated, 481 downregulated).

Functional analysis showed that the VPA-DEPs were significantly enriched in the Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway, and among the pathway genes the one with the greatest expression change was **RNF146** (log2FC = 0.72, p = 1.2×10⁻²). Rnf146 is an E3 ubiquitin ligase that positively regulates Wnt/β-catenin signaling by inducing the degradation of Axin, a core component of the β-catenin destruction complex. Western blotting verified an increase in Rnf146 protein and a decrease in phosphorylated β-catenin (p-β-catenin) in the PFC of VPA mice. In addition, the downregulated VPA-DEPs significantly overlapped with astrocyte marker genes, ASD postmortem-brain DEGs (particularly the astrocyte module M9), and ASD/developmental-delay (DD) risk genes, supporting the pathophysiological validity of the model.

To test causality, Rnf146 was overexpressed via AAV in the PFC of normal adult C57BL/6 mice, which reproduced a severe deficit in social recognition (with only a mild effect on social preference) and reduced p-β-catenin. RNA-seq revealed that in the Rnf146-overexpressing PFC the Wnt signaling pathway was upregulated (increased TCF4 transcription-factor activity, with upregulation of target genes Chn2, Maml2, Phactr2, and Trps1), whereas genes related to synapse organization and memory were downregulated. Electrophysiology (whole-cell patch-clamp) showed significantly increased amplitude and frequency of spontaneous excitatory postsynaptic currents (sEPSCs), while inhibitory currents (sIPSCs) and neuronal excitability were unchanged, indicating that a shift in the excitatory/inhibitory (E/I) balance is the mechanism underlying the social-behavior deficits.

In conclusion, this study is the first to propose that Rnf146 is critical for social behavior and that its upregulation mediates the social deficits of VPA-induced ASD, and it suggests that the Rnf146–Wnt/β-catenin axis can serve as a therapeutic target even in the adult stage.

## Methods & Technologies
- **Animal model**: Pregnant CD1 (ICR) mice were given subcutaneous injections between the scapulae of VPA 350 mg/kg (10 ml/kg) or vehicle (0.9% physiological saline) at embryonic day 10 (E10) to generate VPA-exposed offspring. Proteomics and behavior were assessed at postnatal weeks 6–7, and PFC tissue was collected one week after behavioral testing.
- **Behavioral testing**: Three-chamber social behavior test (social preference + social recognition), with heatmap analysis. Statistics were performed with GraphPad Prism v7 (two-tailed unpaired t-test, two-way ANOVA, Sidak's multiple comparisons).
- **Quantitative proteomics**: PFC protein extraction → trypsin digestion → **TMT isobaric labeling** (8 samples, 24 fractions) → high-pH reversed-phase LC fractionation → high-resolution **Orbitrap LC-MS/MS (DDA)**. Analyzed with **MaxQuant**, followed by quantile normalization, then PCA, correlation, and hierarchical clustering. A total of 5,624 proteins were quantified (median sequence coverage ~17.5%).
- **DEP definition and enrichment analysis**: p < 0.05 was used to define VPA-DEPs. Mouse proteins were converted to human orthologs using the Mouse Genome Informatics homology database. Cell-type specificity was assessed using markers from mouse nervous-system single-cell RNA-seq (Zeisel et al.). Functional mapping and PPI analysis used MSigDB Gene Ontology and Reactome via **EnrichR**.
- **Risk-gene and other-model comparisons**: Overlap analysis with high-confidence risk-gene sets for ASD, DD, EP (epilepsy), and SCZ (schizophrenia); ASD postmortem-brain DEGs (Parikshak et al., WGCNA modules); and proteomics from other ASD models (Cntnap2, Elp2, Pten) (one-sided Fisher's exact test, with OR and FDR).
- **RNA sequencing**: PFC of Rnf146-overexpressing mice. FastQC quality control, quantification with **Salmon** (quasi-mapping, GENCODE vM30/GRCm39). DEG (FDR ≤ 0.1), GSEA, PROGENy pathway-activity scoring, SynGO, and transcription-factor activity inference.
- **Network analysis**: **WGCNA (v1.71)** weighted co-expression network identified 5 modules.
- **Viral vector/surgery**: pAAV-hsyn-mRnf146-T2A-eGFP (control eGFP) was constructed and packaged as AAV2/1. Bilateral stereotaxic injection into the PFC (AP +2.4, ML ±0.5, DV −2.4/−1.8) at 5×10¹² GC/mL. Experiments were performed 4 weeks later.
- **Western blotting**: Antibodies — p-β-catenin (#9561), β-catenin (#9582, Cell Signaling), RNF146 (NBP1-85318, NOVUS), α-Tubulin (sc-8035, Santa Cruz). Normalized to tubulin, with p-β-catenin normalized to β-catenin.
- **Electrophysiology**: Whole-cell patch-clamp to measure sEPSC/sIPSC amplitude and frequency, and action potentials in response to current injection (neuronal excitability).

## Key Genes / Proteins
- **RNF146 / Rnf146**: Ring finger protein 146. A poly(ADP-ribose)-dependent E3 ubiquitin ligase. Positively regulates Wnt/β-catenin signaling by degrading Axin and thereby preventing formation of the β-catenin destruction complex. The central hub protein of this study.
- **β-catenin (CTNNB1)**: The core effector transcriptional co-activator of canonical Wnt signaling.
- **p-β-catenin (phosphorylated β-catenin)**: The form phosphorylated by the destruction complex (GSK3, APC, Axin) and directed toward degradation. Decreased upon VPA exposure and Rnf146 overexpression.
- **Axin**: The concentration-limiting scaffold component of the β-catenin destruction complex. The degradation target of Rnf146.
- **TCF4**: A transcription factor that binds β-catenin to transcriptionally activate Wnt target genes. Its activity increases upon Rnf146 overexpression (upregulation of targets Chn2, Maml2, Phactr2, Trps1) — a key mediator of Wnt activation.
- **Crebbp (CBP)**: A component of a transcriptional-regulatory complex that represses Wnt/β-catenin; an ASD risk gene.
- **Syt1 (Synaptotagmin-1)**: A Wnt target molecule for excitatory synaptic neurotransmitter release.
- **Usp9x**: A ubiquitin-specific peptidase that regulates canonical and noncanonical Wnt signaling via deubiquitination of DVL2.
- **Gpc3 (Glypican-3)**: Regulates Wnt–Frizzled interaction; a Wnt-pathway gene among the Rnf146-DEGs.
- **Ube3b**: An intellectual-disability-associated E3 ubiquitin ligase disrupted by Rnf146 (module 2, eigengene connectivity 0.96).
- **Chrd (Chordin)**: A component of BMP signaling, decreased in the Rnf146-overexpressing PFC.
- **Presynaptic membrane gene set**: Ano1, Ano2, Htr1a, Htr7, Kcna4, Ngfr (upregulated Rnf146-DEGs, enriched for presynaptic membrane). Excitatory-related genes such as **Slc17a7 (VGLUT1)** were downregulated in module 2.

## Main Findings
- **Behavior**: VPA-exposed mice lost both social preference and social recognition in the three-chamber test, with a significantly reduced preference index (PI) (P < 0.0001). VPA offspring showed reduced litter size and a crooked-tail phenotype due to neural tube defects, and there was a significant correlation between the social preference index and the tail-curvature angle.
- **Proteome**: 5,624 proteins quantified; VPA vs control were clearly separated by PCA (lowest Pearson correlation 98.7%). 941 VPA-DEPs (460 upregulated / 481 downregulated).
- **Cell-type/disease overlap**: Downregulated VPA-DEPs were enriched for astrocyte markers (OR = 3.6, FDR = 1.9×10⁻⁴) and for human developing-brain astrocytes (OR = 3.7, FDR = 9.0×10⁻⁶). They overlapped with the ASD postmortem-brain astrocyte module M9 (OR = 2.4, FDR = 2.6×10⁻³) and were enriched for ASD risk genes (OR = 3.0, FDR = 8.4×10⁻³) and DD risk genes (OR = 2.4, FDR = 2.7×10⁻²). They were also associated with the neurodegeneration KEGG pathway (hsa05022, OR = 15.5, p = 1.9×10⁻³).
- **Wnt-pathway enrichment**: Signaling by WNT (OR = 1.8, FDR = 4.0×10⁻²), TCF-dependent signaling (OR = 2.2, FDR = 2.0×10⁻²). Downregulated DEPs were enriched for the β-catenin phosphorylation cascade (OR = 2.4, FDR = 7.8×10⁻⁶) and for β-catenin degradation by the destruction complex (OR = 7.9, FDR = 1.1×10⁻²). Among the pathway genes, **RNF146 showed the greatest expression disturbance** (log2FC = 0.72, p = 1.2×10⁻²).
- **Validation (Western blot)**: In VPA PFC, Rnf146 was significantly increased (P = 0.0312) and p-β-catenin significantly decreased (P = 0.0185), while total β-catenin showed an increasing trend without a significant difference (P = 0.1577).
- **Causal reproduction**: Overexpressing Rnf146 in the normal adult PFC reproduced a severe deficit in social recognition (with only a mild effect on social preference) and reduced p-β-catenin. Baseline locomotion and anxiety were normal.
- **Transcriptome**: 294 Rnf146-DEGs (FDR ≤ 0.1), overlapping with 11 VPA-DEPs (Adgrb2, Chrm1, Fmnl1, Islr2, Mtmr2, Plppr1, Ripor2, Rnf146, Smpd4, Tmem132a, Trir). Enriched for positive Wnt signaling (GO:0030177, NES = 1.7, FDR = 4.9×10⁻²), with upregulated Wnt in pathway-activity scoring (NES = 1.5, p = 1.2×10⁻³). WGCNA module 1 (Wnt/neurodevelopment) was enriched for upregulated DEGs (OR = 47.3, FDR = 9.2×10⁻¹⁰⁸), and module 2 (synapse organization/memory) was enriched for downregulated DEGs.
- **Electrophysiology**: In Rnf146-overexpressing neurons, sEPSC amplitude (P = 0.0027) and frequency (P = 0.025) were significantly increased, while sIPSCs and neuronal excitability were unchanged → a shift in E/I balance.

## Applications
- Provides a proteome-level molecular pathophysiology map of a prenatal-drug (VPA)-exposure autism model, presenting a novel molecular pathway (Rnf146–Wnt/β-catenin) that cross-validates with ASD postmortem brain and risk genes.
- Proposes the Rnf146–Wnt/β-catenin axis as a candidate therapeutic target for social deficits at the adult stage (indicating the possibility of intervention not only during development but also in adulthood).
- Serves as an example of a multi-omics validation pipeline that proceeds from quantitative proteomics → integration with risk-gene and postmortem-brain data → causal validation via AAV genetic manipulation → mechanistic elucidation by electrophysiology.

## Extensibility / Future Directions
- This study used only male mice (because VPA has less effect on female social behavior, and Rnf146 has been reported as a male-specific ASD candidate). Elucidating the **effect of Rnf146 overexpression on social behavior in the female PFC** is needed.
- Individual causal elucidation of the Rnf146→TCF4→Wnt target-gene axis, and of changes in presynaptic membrane genes and synapse-organization genes.
- Therapeutic validation of alleviating social deficits via Rnf146 inhibition / Wnt-signaling normalization (e.g., Axin stabilization, tankyrase inhibition).
- Cell-type-specific follow-up studies on whether the reduction of astrocyte-specific proteins contributes to synaptic and behavioral deficits.

## Disease Relevance
- Presents dysregulation of Wnt/β-catenin signaling as a molecular mechanism of ASD, particularly the prenatal-drug-exposure type of ASD. Whereas Wnt is activated in the VPA model, it is downregulated in Fmr1⁻/⁻ and GSK3β models, suggesting that **bidirectional dysregulation of Wnt signaling** is involved in ASD pathogenesis.
- Rnf146 has previously been studied mainly in cancer and neurodegeneration such as Parkinson's disease, but this study presents the first direct link to social behavior and ASD.
- Supports excitatory/inhibitory (E/I) imbalance — an increase in excitatory synaptic transmission — as the cellular mechanism of social deficits.

## Novelty vs. Prior Work
- One of the **first studies to characterize the PFC proteome of VPA-exposed mice by TMT quantitative proteomics**, differentiating it from prior transcriptome-centered studies and studies of other brain regions.
- Identifies **Rnf146 as the upstream mediator of Wnt/β-catenin activation and directly validates causality by AAV overexpression** (going beyond simple correlational observation).
- **First to show that Rnf146 regulates social behavior**. By demonstrating that the proteome DEPs significantly overlap with ASD postmortem-brain DEGs and risk genes, it strengthens the model's relevance to human pathology.
- When compared with other ASD models (Cntnap2, Elp2, Pten), the downregulated VPA-DEPs overlapped with those models' upregulated DEPs, revealing an **opposite-direction pathophysiology**.
