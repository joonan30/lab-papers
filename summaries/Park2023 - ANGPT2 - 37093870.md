# Angiopoietin-2–Dependent Spatial Vascular Destabilization Promotes T-cell Exclusion and Limits Immunotherapy in Melanoma

## One-Line Summary
Endothelial overexpression of ANGPT2 (angiopoietin-2) at the melanoma tumor periphery suppresses TIE2 signaling to render vessels unstable and leaky, which excludes CD8⁺ T cells from the tumor core and thereby limits antitumor immunity and anti-PD-1 immune checkpoint therapy; blocking ANGPT2 restores T-cell infiltration, identifying it as a promising target to enhance immunotherapy efficacy.

## Research Summary
Within the tumor microenvironment (TME), the spatial position of T cells determines their capacity to contact target cells and kill tumors. In particular, "T-cell exclusion," in which CD8⁺ T cells fail to enter the tumor parenchyma and remain confined to the periphery, is associated with immunotherapy refractoriness (non-response), yet the biology generating this distinctive spatial distribution was unclear. Among patients with metastatic melanoma, 30–50% show de novo (primary) resistance to immune checkpoint inhibitors, and 20–30% progress to secondary resistance after an initial response. The authors hypothesized that ANGPT2, a vascular destabilizing factor, is a key mediator of this T-cell exclusion.

Using genetically defined syngeneic mouse melanoma models (YUMMER1.7, YUMM1.7, B16-F10) together with human melanoma tissue, the team quantitatively analyzed the spatial expression of ANGPT2 across whole tumor cross-sections alongside T-cell distribution, vascular integrity, and immunotherapy response. CD8⁺ T-cell exclusion spatially coincided with ANGPT2 overexpression and increased vascular leakage at the tumor periphery. In the TCGA-SKCM cohort and in human melanoma single-cell RNA-seq data, tumors with higher ANGPT2 expression had significantly higher "exclusion-up" gene signatures, and ANGPT2 was most highly expressed by endothelial cells.

Blocking ANGPT2 with a dual approach — pharmacologic (the selective anti-ANGPT2 antibody REGN910) and genetic (endothelial cell–specific Angpt2 deletion, Angpt2^iΔEC) — released CD8⁺ T cells from the periphery and restored their infiltration into the tumor core (91% increase in core CD8⁺ T cells in YUMMER1.7, and a 98% increase with genetic deletion). Mechanistically, ANGPT2 blockade activated TIE2 signaling (total TIE2 and p-TIE2) at the tumor periphery, raised vascular integrity markers (desmin⁺ pericytes, VE-cadherin, claudin-5, type IV collagen), and reduced endothelial adhesion molecules (VCAM-1, L-selectin). These changes were especially pronounced at the periphery (spatially selective).

ANGPT2 targeting was accompanied by increased apoptosis (cleaved caspase-3) and decreased proliferation (Ki-67), delaying tumor growth in melanomas of differing immunogenicity (highly immunogenic YUMMER1.7 and poorly immunogenic YUMM1.7). This antitumor effect was CD8⁺ T-cell dependent — in immunodeficient SCID mice, ANGPT2 blockade did not suppress tumor growth (although vascular leakage was still reduced), and depleting CD8⁺ T cells with anti-CD8α abolished the effect. Furthermore, reversing T-cell exclusion converted anti-PD-1–refractory melanoma (YUMM1.7) to responsive and further enhanced the response of responsive melanoma (YUMMER1.7). Combining anti-ANGPT2 with anti-PD-1 markedly increased core CD8⁺ T-cell infiltration, functional (granzyme B⁺) T cells, and apoptosis relative to either monotherapy.

In conclusion, ANGPT2/TIE2 signaling is a central axis that drives T-cell exclusion via vascular destabilization at the tumor periphery, and ANGPT2 blockade is a promising target for potentially enhancing immune checkpoint inhibitor efficacy.

## Methods & Technologies
- **Mouse melanoma models**:
  - YUMMER1.7 (Braf^V600E, Pten^−/−, Cdkn2a^−/−; carrying additional somatic mutations from UVB irradiation → highly immunogenic, anti-PD-1 responsive). Recapitulates the clinically relevant phenotype of "peripheral CD8⁺ T-cell retention (core exclusion)."
  - YUMM1.7 (parental line, poorly immunogenic, anti-PD-1 refractory).
  - B16-F10 (lung metastasis model; lungs harvested 3 weeks after intravenous injection).
  - Subcutaneous inoculation (e.g., 2.5×10⁵ YUMMER1.7 or 5×10⁵ YUMM1.7); treatment initiated at tumor volume ~70 mm³; euthanasia at tumor >2,000 mm³.
- **Genetic model**: VE-cadherin-CreER^T2 × Angpt2^flox/flox = **Angpt2^iΔEC** (tamoxifen-induced endothelial cell–specific Angpt2 deletion, 4 consecutive daily intraperitoneal injections).
- **Mouse strains**: C57BL/6 (immunocompetent), CB17-SCID (T-cell and B-cell deficient).
- **Antibody/drug treatments**: selective anti-ANGPT2 antibody **REGN910** 12.5 mg/kg every 3 days (control human IgG REGN1945), anti-PD-1 100 μg/mouse every 2 days, anti-CD8α for CD8⁺ T-cell depletion, and combination therapy (REGN910 + anti-PD-1).
- **Immunofluorescence / immunohistochemistry (IHC)**: cardiac perfusion fixation (1% PFA), 30% sucrose, OCT embedding, 50 μm sections. Targets — ANGPT2, TIE2, p-TIE2 (phosphorylated), FOXO1 (nuclear/cytoplasmic), vascular leakage marker fibrin(ogen), vascular stability markers (desmin, VE-cadherin, claudin-5, type IV collagen), adhesion molecules (ICAM-1, VCAM-1, L-selectin, P-selectin), CD31/VE-cadherin (vessels), CD8, Ki-67, cleaved-activated caspase-3.
- **Flow cytometry**: tumor core and periphery separately dissociated, collagenase IV/DNase treatment, RBC lysis, live/dead staining, then surface and intracellular (granzyme B, Foxp3) staining. Acquired on Novocyte Quanteon (Agilent), analyzed in FlowJo v10.8.1. Analyzed CD45⁺, CD8⁺, CD4⁺, Treg (CD4⁺Foxp3⁺), TAM (M1: CD206^low CD11c^high / M2: CD206^high CD11c^low), TEM, and M-MDSC/PMN-MDSC.
- **Morphometric analysis**: Axio Observer 7 + Apotome, whole tumor cross-section imaging. Distance maps from the tumor margin computed with the **MATLAB bwdist function** → CD8⁺ T cells quantified as a function of distance. Periphery (<500 μm from margin) vs. core (>500 μm), cells per mm². Quantification of necrosis, proliferation (Ki-67), apoptosis (caspase-3), vessel density (CD31 or VE-cadherin⁺ area %), and leakage (fibrin).
- **Human specimens**: pretreatment metastatic melanoma frozen tissue from the HICCC Tissue Bank, and human melanoma brain metastasis tissue (immunostaining).
- **Single-cell RNA-seq**: human melanoma 33-tumor dataset **GSE115978** (analysis code based on Jerby-Arnon GitHub). Derived cell-type–specific genes (malignant cells n=389, CD8⁺ T cells n=50).
- **Bulk RNA-seq**: **TCGA-SKCM, 474 cutaneous melanomas** (xenabrowser RSEM normalized, log₂). Estimated CD8⁺ T-cell abundance (OE) and stratified by ANGPT2 expression into tertiles (>90th n=48 / 50–90th n=189 / <50th n=237).
- **T-cell exclusion signature gene definition**: Pearson correlation between malignant-cell genes and CD8⁺ T-cell OE → selected 20 seed exclusion-up and 20 exclusion-down genes → prioritized individual genes by partial Spearman correlation. Defined programs of 225 exclusion-up and 162 exclusion-down genes (Wilcoxon rank-sum).
- **RT-qPCR**: RNeasy extraction, qScript cDNA, PerfeCTa SYBR Green, QuantStudio 5. GAPDH internal control.
- **Statistics**: mean ± SEM. Paired/unpaired two-tailed Student t test, Mann–Whitney (tumor growth endpoint), one-way ANOVA + Tukey (multi-group comparison), Wilcoxon rank-sum (RNA-seq). GraphPad Prism 9.1.0.

## Key Genes / Proteins
- **ANGPT2 (angiopoietin-2)**: central factor of this study. Endothelial-secreted vascular destabilizing factor; context-dependent agonist/antagonist of TIE2. Overexpressed at the tumor periphery, driving T-cell exclusion and vascular leakage.
- **TIE2 (TEK)**: endothelial receptor tyrosine kinase, key regulator of angiogenesis and vascular stabilization. Activated (stabilizing) upon ANGPT1 binding; inhibited when ANGPT2 competes.
- **p-TIE2 (phosphorylated TIE2)**: active form of TIE2. Increases upon ANGPT2 blockade and serves as an indicator of vascular stabilization.
- **ANGPT1 (angiopoietin-1)**: TIE2 agonist, induces vascular maturation and stabilization. A decreased ANGPT2/ANGPT1 ratio indicates vascular stability.
- **FOXO1 (forkhead box O1)**: transcription factor. When TIE2/PI3K/Akt signaling is suppressed, it translocates to the nucleus and becomes active, upregulating ANGPT2 expression (positive feedback). Nuclear FOXO1 is high in peripheral vessels.
- **VE-cadherin, claudin-5, desmin, type IV collagen**: markers of endothelial junction, pericyte, and basement membrane integrity. Increased upon ANGPT2 blockade.
- **VCAM-1, ICAM-1, L-selectin, P-selectin**: endothelial adhesion molecules. VCAM-1 and L-selectin in particular decrease at the periphery upon ANGPT2 blockade.
- **CD8 / CD4 / Foxp3 (Treg) / granzyme B**: T-cell and functional T-cell markers.
- **VEGF / VEGFR-2**: established anti-angiogenic therapy targets (comparison context).
- **SOX4, EIF3H (exclusion-up); PERP, IFI27L2/IRF-related (exclusion-down)**: example genes of the T-cell exclusion program signature.

## Main Findings
- **Spatial coincidence of T-cell exclusion with peripheral ANGPT2 and vascular leakage**: YUMMER1.7 tumors have necrosis of only ~2.5% of the total, and a substantial fraction of CD8⁺ T cells is retained at the periphery (<500 μm). Peripheral vessels show significantly higher ANGPT2 expression (%CD31) than the core (P=0.002), higher nuclear FOXO1 (P<0.0001), and higher fibrin leakage (P=0.0005).
- **Human and TCGA validation**: in human melanoma and brain metastases, strong ANGPT2 plus CD8 accumulation at the periphery. In TCGA-SKCM, the ANGPT2-high group (>90th) has significantly higher exclusion-up and lower exclusion-down signatures than the ANGPT2-low group (<50th) (both P<0.0001). ANGPT2 is expressed at highest levels in endothelial cells.
- **ANGPT2 blockade → restored core T-cell infiltration**: REGN910 treatment increased core CD8⁺ T cells by **91%** in YUMMER1.7, by **75%** in the poorly immunogenic YUMM1.7, and by **98%** with genetic deletion (Angpt2^iΔEC). The effect was specific to CD8⁺ and CD4⁺ T cells, with a decreased Treg (CD4⁺Foxp3⁺) fraction and an increased M1/M2 TAM ratio. TAM, TEM, and MDSC frequencies were largely unchanged.
- **TIE2 signaling reactivation and vascular stabilization**: with ANGPT2 blockade, TIE2 expression increased 3.9-fold at the periphery and 3.7-fold at the core, and p-TIE2 increased 8.8-fold at the periphery (2.4-fold at the core), stronger at the periphery. desmin⁺ pericyte coverage increased 2.7-fold (periphery) / 1.9-fold (core), VE-cadherin 4.8/2.3-fold, claudin-5 2.8/1.5-fold, and type IV collagen 1.2-fold. Overall tumor vessel density decreased, and fibrin leakage decreased **61%** at the periphery and 20% at the core. CXCL9/10/11 chemokines were unchanged (not chemokine-mediated).
- **Tumor growth inhibition with increased apoptosis and decreased proliferation**: significant growth delay in YUMMER1.7 (P=0.0004), increased cleaved caspase-3 (P=0.003), decreased Ki-67 (P=0.009). YUMM1.7 also delayed (P=0.0006), and Angpt2^iΔEC also delayed (P=0.002).
- **CD8⁺ T-cell dependence**: in SCID mice, ANGPT2 blockade had no effect on tumor growth or apoptosis (although vascular leakage was still reduced). Depleting CD8⁺ T cells with anti-CD8α abolished the antitumor effect in both YUMM1.7 and YUMMER1.7 → the antitumor effect is CD8⁺ T-cell mediated.
- **Synergy with anti-PD-1 combination**: in anti-PD-1–refractory YUMM1.7, the combination significantly improved over anti-PD-1 alone (P=0.0003) and also over anti-ANGPT2 alone (P=0.02). In responsive YUMMER1.7, the combination was best. With the combination, core CD8⁺ infiltration (36% increase in YUMM1.7, 57% increase in YUMMER1.7), granzyme B⁺ functional T cells, and apoptosis were maximal.

## Applications
- Provides rationale that targeting the ANGPT2/TIE2 axis with antibodies (e.g., the REGN910 class) combined with immune checkpoint inhibitors (such as anti-PD-1) can stabilize peripheral tumor vessels and direct CD8⁺ T cells into the tumor core, thereby enhancing immunotherapy efficacy.
- Preclinical rationale for combination strategies that could convert "cold/non-infiltrated" melanomas — in which T cells are excluded to the periphery — and immune checkpoint inhibitor–refractory patients to responsive.
- Because tumor-infiltrating CD8⁺ T-cell density is a predictor of immune checkpoint inhibitor response, ANGPT2 blockade acts to improve this predictive biomarker (degree of infiltration).

## Extensibility / Future Directions
- Clinical connections noted in the paper: early clinical trials of ANGPT2-specific antibody + immune checkpoint inhibitor combinations (phase I: NCT02141542, NCT03239145), CTLA-4 + ANGPT2 blockade (MEDI3617) with acceptable safety in advanced melanoma phase I, and atezolizumab + ANGPT2 inhibition (hepatocellular carcinoma) → extensible to solid tumors beyond melanoma.
- Core T-cell density was heterogeneous by region yet independent of necrosis → additional mechanisms determining intratumoral T-cell position need to be defined (noted by the authors).
- Differences in immune regulation depending on the cellular source of ANGPT2 (mainly endothelial, partly tumor cells), and regulation via hypoxia (HIF) and the FOXO1 positive feedback loop, need to be clarified.
- Auxiliary antitumor contributions of CD4⁺ T cells, and validation in the distinct TMEs of primary versus metastatic tumors, are needed.
- Whether ANGPT2 acts directly on immune cells (e.g., TEM recruitment) beyond the indirect pathway of vascular destabilization requires further study.

## Disease Relevance
Melanoma (which accounts for most skin cancer deaths) is highly immunogenic, yet many patients resist immune checkpoint inhibitors. This study demonstrates that T-cell exclusion, one axis of that resistance, originates from ANGPT2-mediated vascular destabilization, and proposes ANGPT2 blockade as a combination target to enhance immunotherapy (anti-PD-1) efficacy. Validation in models bearing clinically relevant driver mutations (Braf, Pten, Cdkn2a) confers strong translational implications for human melanoma and provides rationale for precision combination therapies that target vascular–immune interactions in the tumor microenvironment.

## Novelty vs. Prior Work
- Existing anti-angiogenic therapy mainly targets VEGF/VEGFR; VEGF inhibition was approached via the "vascular normalization" concept along with selective regression of immature vessels, but had limitations of resistance and metastatic recurrence. This study is the first to mechanistically define that the ANGPT2/TIE2 axis — known as a compensatory signal for VEGF resistance — drives T-cell exclusion **spatially (periphery-selective)**.
- Unlike prior melanoma vascular and therapy studies that relied mainly on the B16 model (which does not recapitulate the mutational burden and immunogenicity of human melanoma), this work used YUMM1.7/YUMMER1.7, which carry clinically relevant driver mutations plus UVB-derived somatic mutations, to recapitulate the spatial T-cell distribution of human melanoma.
- Dual validation, pharmacologic (REGN910) and genetic (Angpt2^iΔEC), whole tumor cross-section distance-based spatial quantification (MATLAB bwdist), and linkage to TCGA and single-cell RNA-seq exclusion signatures established the causal and spatial relationship between ANGPT2 and T-cell exclusion at multiple levels.
- Clearly showing that the antitumor effect of ANGPT2 blockade is CD8⁺ T-cell dependent (SCID and CD8 depletion) and providing combination rationale for converting anti-PD-1–refractory tumors to responsive is novel.
